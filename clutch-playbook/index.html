The Big Idea

Why this system exists and why it works

The Problem

Clutch reviews have ~30 questions. Nobody wants to sit down and type paragraphs about your agency. The friction is enormous. You ask, they say "yeah sure", and then it never happens.

The Solution

You do the work for them. Book a 15-minute video call, ask them the Clutch questions conversationally, record it in 4K, transcribe their answers, paraphrase into Clutch format, and send it back saying "here's what you already said, just copy and paste."

One call = Three assets. From a single 15-minute conversation you get: a video case study (for proposals + website), a written testimonial, and a Clutch review. The hardest thing is getting people on video. Once they're on, you extract everything.

Riverside.fm Setup Guide

How to record client calls in 4K like a pro

Why Riverside (Not Zoom)

Riverside records locally on each person's device, then uploads the raw files. This means:

  • 4K video quality (up to 2160p) regardless of internet speed
  • No connection dropouts in the recording, even if their WiFi stutters
  • Separate audio tracks per person (critical for editing)
  • No software install for the guest -- browser-based
  • Built-in transcription you can export immediately

1. Create Your Studio

Go to riverside.fm, create a free or paid account. Create a new Studio for "Client Feedback Calls." Enable video recording + transcription.

2. Your Camera

For 4K, you need a 4K webcam (Logitech Brio, Elgato Facecam) or a DSLR/mirrorless with HDMI capture card. The client's webcam quality is on them.

3. Your Audio

Use a dedicated USB mic (Blue Yeti, Rode NT-USB) or a lapel mic. Wired headphones recommended to prevent echo. Never rely on laptop mic.

4. Lighting

Face a window (natural light) or use a ring light / key light. Avoid overhead-only lighting. The client doesn't need to worry about this.

5. Invite the Client

Share the studio link via email. They click it, enter their name, allow camera/mic, and they're in. No account required for guests.

6. After the Call

Riverside processes the recordings (may take a few minutes). Download 4K video + transcript. Export separate audio tracks if needed for editing.

Cost: Riverside has a free tier (limited recordings/hours). The Standard plan (~$15/mo) is enough for client review calls. Business plan for teams.

What the Client Sees on Clutch

So you can walk them through it if they get stuck

Step 1: Create an Account / Sign In

The client goes to review.clutch.co and either signs in or creates a new account. They can use:

  • Google (fastest)
  • LinkedIn
  • Work email (First Name, Last Name, Work Email, Password)

Note: Personal email addresses (Gmail, Yahoo) may be flagged. They should use their company email for credibility.

Step 2: Choose Review Format

After signing in, they see two options:

  • Classic Review Form -- Traditional question-and-answer format. This is the one we want. They copy-paste our pre-filled answers into each field.
  • AI-Powered Chat (Beta) -- DO NOT USE -- This rewrites everything with generic AI language and ignores what they actually said. It defeats the entire purpose of our system. Always use Classic Review Form.

Step 3: Fill In + Submit

They paste your pre-written answers into each section, add their star ratings, and hit submit. With pre-filled answers, this takes about 5 minutes.

IMPORTANT: Tell them to choose "Classic Review Form" -- NEVER the AI chat. The AI chat will rewrite their words into generic fluff. Our whole system is built on using their real words from the call. Classic form = paste and done.

Pre-Call Checklist

Run through this before every client feedback call

  • Riverside studio created with video recording + transcription enabled
  • Camera working in 4K / highest quality available
  • Mic tested with headphones (no echo)
  • Lighting good -- face lit, no backlight, no weird shadows
  • Questions printed/visible on a second screen or notepad
  • Client name + project details reviewed so you can prompt naturally
  • Studio link sent to client with calendar invite
  • Quiet environment -- no background noise, door closed

After the Call

The post-call workflow to turn one call into three assets

Transcription + Paraphrasing (30-60 min)

  1. Download the transcript from Riverside
  2. Map answers to Clutch sections -- Background, Challenge, Solution, Results
  3. Clean up the language -- remove filler words ("um," "like," "you know"), fix grammar, tighten sentences
  4. Keep their voice -- don't rewrite everything. Use their exact phrasing where it's strong. Only paraphrase where they were repetitive or rambling
  5. Pull the best quotes -- identify 2-3 one-liners for your testimonial library
  6. Format into the copy-paste email -- organize by Clutch section with clear labels

Video Editing (30-60 min)

  1. Download the 4K footage from Riverside
  2. Cut a 2-3 minute highlight reel -- best moments, specific results, genuine reactions
  3. Add lower third with client name + title + company
  4. Add your logo intro/outro
  5. Export in 1080p for web, keep 4K master for future use
Time investment: ~15 min call + ~60 min post-production = 1.5 hours total per client for 3 high-value assets. Compare that to begging for reviews that never arrive.

Pro Tips

On the Call

  • Start with small talk -- 1-2 minutes to warm them up before recording
  • Tell them what you're doing -- "I'm going to ask you a few questions about our project, just talk naturally, and I'll handle the rest"
  • Don't read questions robotically -- have a conversation, use their answers to segue into the next topic
  • Prompt for specifics -- "You mentioned a 30% increase, do you remember roughly what that translated to in revenue?"
  • Let silences sit -- people often fill silence with their best answers
  • End with: "Anything else you'd want other business owners to know?" -- this often produces the best testimonial quote

Getting More Reviews

  • Best time to ask: right after a win (big result, successful launch, positive feedback)
  • Build it into onboarding: "At the 90-day mark, we'll do a quick feedback call" -- set the expectation upfront
  • For Clutch Gold Verified status: you need 20 reviews. Start collecting now.
  • Reviews with zero criticism look fake. The "what could we improve?" question is important. A small piece of constructive feedback makes the whole review more credible.
  • Phone reviews (by a Clutch analyst) are available for projects over $25K. These carry more weight but you have less control. Prefer the online/self-submit method.
