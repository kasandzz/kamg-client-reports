Strategic Reframes for Russ

The five things the team probably believes that aren't quite right — said in the order they should be said in the call.

A. LTV is the math, not CAC.

3x ROAS at $15K AOV isn't a media problem. If we shift program economics from $15K LTV to $22K (LP attach 25%, MA attach 10%), 3x ROAS is achievable at current CAC. The team is treating ROAS as a media metric. It's a lifecycle metric.

B. The sales team is the cheapest media buy.

Spending another $50K on Meta to lower CAC 8% is harder than coaching the bottom-quartile EC up 4 points. This week's #1 ROAS lever is sales-floor coaching, not media. Reframe the war room around close rate.

C. Workshop = qualification, not conversion.

Workshop's job is to identify and route, not to close. The application + EC call is the close. Workshop optimization plateaus; the real leak is application-to-close (which we don't measure cleanly yet). That measurement is P0.

D. Friday VIP is the most underleveraged asset.

It's the only live moment in the funnel. 1-to-many psychology + live close energy. Build the entire VIP upsell ladder around making Friday unmissable, not "an extra Q&A." Stop framing Friday as a bonus — frame the workshop as the bonus.

E. We don't have a "name the regulator" creative in test.

Cross-niche ICP research (therapists / attorneys / coaches / educators / CPAs) is unanimous: the regulatory frame earns 30 seconds of attention. Top-of-funnel creative should include one variant per niche naming the licensure body (state bar, AICPA, licensing board). Cheap test, big read on hook efficacy.
