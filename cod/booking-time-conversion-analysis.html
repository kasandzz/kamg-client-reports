Verdict
Next-day morning is the higher-quality booking slot. 20.5-point lower reschedule rate (34.4% vs 54.9%). More than half of same-day afternoon bookings get rescheduled -- classic impulse booking that doesn't hold. Next-day AM prospects have slept on it and made a deliberate choice.

The nuance: Same-day PM has a lower raw no-show rate (1.7% vs 6.6%), but that's because same-day bookers who don't plan to show just reschedule instead. Next-day bookers are more likely to hard no-show -- they committed but life happened. The net result still favors next-day AM: fewer total falloffs.

Reschedule Rate as Commitment Proxy

With only 3 "Showed" records in 3 months, reschedule rate (586 events) is the strongest available signal. Lower reschedule = higher commitment to the original booking.

Most Committed (Lowest Reschedule)

  • Next-day Morning (8-11am): 34.4% reschedule across 151 bookings. These prospects slept on it and decided deliberately.
  • Next-day Afternoon (12-7pm): 37.7% across 289 bookings. Still solid commitment.
  • 2-4 Days Out: 37.6% across 681 bookings. Largest sample, moderate commitment.

Flakiest (Highest Reschedule)

  • 5+ Days Out: 74.1% reschedule -- 3 out of 4 bookings get moved. Too far out, life intervenes.
  • Same-day Morning: 59.2% -- rushed booking, not mentally ready for the call.
  • Same-day Afternoon: 54.9% -- impulse booking post-workshop, over half reschedule.
The Impulse Trap
Same-day bookings look great on paper -- "hot lead, book them now!" -- but the data shows 55-59% reschedule. These prospects are emotionally activated but haven't made a real commitment. By contrast, next-day morning prospects have processed the workshop overnight and made a deliberate choice. They're 20 points more likely to keep their booking.

Data Gaps & Limitations

Critical "Showed" Status Almost Non-Existent
  • Only 3 of 1,387 bookings (0.2%) have "Showed" in Jan-Apr 2026
  • 533 bookings (38%) still sit at default "Booked" -- never updated
  • 586 bookings (42%) marked "Rescheduled" -- unknown if they eventually showed
  • Impact: Cannot calculate show rates or enrollment rates. This report is limited to reschedule/cancel/no-show patterns.
  • Fix: Backfill from Zoom attendance data (Cloud Function already deployed). Write a job that matches Zoom attendee emails to booking emails and updates status.
Critical Enrollment Tracking Disconnected
  • Stripe tracks $27 workshop tickets, not high-ticket enrollment (only 7 email matches)
  • High-ticket payments go through a separate processor or Stripe account not in the warehouse
  • Impact: Cannot answer "which booking time leads to more enrollments"
  • Fix: Connect enrollment data source (separate Stripe account, GHL pipeline "Enrolled" stage, or Authorize.net) to booking emails
Important "Pending" Records May Hide Signal
  • 533 bookings (38%) are still "Booked" -- some of these are future calls, but many are past calls with no status update
  • Next-day Morning has the highest "Pending" rate (50%) -- if these resolve favorably, this segment is even stronger than it appears
  • Same-day segments have lower pending rates (29-35%) because more of their outcomes get recorded as reschedules
Note Timezone Unconfirmed
  • Call times from strings like "Friday, November 7, 2025 7:00 PM" -- timezone not specified in the data
  • Assumed Eastern Time. If stored in UTC, the hourly analysis shifts by 4-5 hours.

Recommended Actions

P0

Fix Booking Status Tracking

Automate "Showed" / "No Show" updates by matching Zoom attendance to booking emails. The Zoom Cloud Function is already deployed and syncing every 6h. Build the status-update job. Without this, all conversion analysis is blind.

P0

Connect Enrollment Data

High-ticket enrollment needs a linkable data source. Whether it's a separate Stripe account, GHL pipeline stage, or Authorize.net -- get it into the warehouse and join to booking emails. This unlocks the revenue question Aar is asking.

P1

Calendar Slot Optimization

Based on current data: favor 8-10am next-day slots in the booking calendar. Consider reducing same-day availability or adding a confirmation step for same-day bookings. Test as A/B on the booking page.

P1

Limit 5+ Day Booking Windows

74% of bookings 5+ days out get rescheduled. Consider capping the booking calendar to 4 days max, or adding aggressive SMS/email nurture sequences for bookings beyond 3 days.

P2

Backfill Historical Status

Run a one-time backfill: match the 533 "Booked" records against Zoom logs + Stripe + GHL to resolve their actual outcomes. This would more than 10x the usable sample size.
