Reschedule Rate as Commitment Proxy

With only 34 "Showed" records, the reschedule rate (829 events) is the most statistically reliable signal we have. Lower reschedule = higher commitment to the original booking.

Lowest Reschedule (Most Committed)

  • Next-day Morning (8-11am): 34.0% reschedule rate across 212 bookings
  • Next-day Afternoon (12-7pm): 37.8% reschedule rate across 444 bookings
  • 2-4 Days Out: 39.0% across the largest sample (910)

Highest Reschedule (Flakiest)

  • 5+ Days Out: 68.5% reschedule -- too far, life gets in the way
  • Same-day Morning: 61.4% -- rushed, not ready
  • Same-day Afternoon: 53.2% -- impulse booking, low follow-through
05

Data Gaps & Limitations

Critical Enrollment Cannot Be Tracked
  • Stripe only has 7 email matches with booking data -- it tracks $27 workshop tickets, not high-ticket enrollment
  • High-ticket payments go through a separate processor or Stripe account not in the warehouse
  • Impact: We can answer "who showed up" but NOT "who enrolled" by booking time
  • Fix: Need enrollment data source (separate Stripe account, GHL pipeline stage, or manual tracker) linked to booking emails
Critical Showed Status Severely Under-Tracked
  • Only 34 of 1,944 bookings (1.7%) have "Showed" status
  • 702 bookings (36%) still sit at default "Booked" status -- never updated
  • 829 bookings (43%) marked "Rescheduled" or "Booked (Rescheduled)" -- unclear if they eventually showed
  • Impact: Show rates are directional only (sample sizes of 2-75 per segment)
  • Fix: Automate status updates from Zoom attendance data (Cloud Function already deployed). Backfill historical "Booked" records using Zoom meeting logs.
Important Timezone Ambiguity
  • Call times parsed from strings like "Friday, November 7, 2025 7:00 PM" -- timezone not specified
  • Assumed Eastern Time based on COD operations, but could be UTC or prospect-local
  • Could shift hour-level analysis by 1-5 hours depending on source system
