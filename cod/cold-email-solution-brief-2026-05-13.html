The problem Russ raised

The root cause is structural, not creative. Cold email lands in inboxes of brick-and-mortar therapists and coaches who have zero prior touch with COD. Reply rate is fine (about 2.7% per the last report, above the 1 to 2% benchmark). The problem is that "reply" to "booked call" to "sales-ready" was built for warm Facebook traffic, and cold prospects skip the indoctrination ad sequence that primed the original model.

The new flow

From one-call close to two-call close.

The current pipeline has a gap between "Franzi books call" and "sales rep gets a stranger on Zoom." The fix is to insert a 15-minute triage call plus an AI-personalized indoctrination deck before the sales call ever happens.

The eight solution layers

What we agreed to build.

Listed in priority order. Layers 1, 2, and 3 do the heavy lifting on Russ's "no idea what we do" concern. Layers 4 through 8 are supporting infrastructure.

01
Two-step process replaces one-call close
Owner: Franzi + sales team

The 15-minute triage call sits between the reply and the sales call. Framed as a free audit or consult, no strings attached. Prospect just shows up and talks about their business. They are then either qualified to a proposal call or moved to the nurture list.

This is the single biggest structural change. COD has never run a two-step model and the team is not currently set up for it. Franzi flagged that the 15-minute call has to be genuinely valuable, which means the person running it needs consult-level competence, not setter-level.

Origin: Dorian's recommendation at 5:01, reinforced by Franzi at 48:00. Pattern is "free estimate" or "free audit" style cold outreach close.
02
AI-personalized pre-indoctrination deck between calls
Owner: Kas

Franzi's 15-minute call gets transcribed. Transcript pipes into Claude, which generates a prompt, which generates a Gamma deck personalized to the prospect's exact niche. Deck explains what a high-ticket offer looks like specifically for trauma coaching, marriage counseling, financial coaching, whatever they came in with. Sent to the prospect between the triage call and the sales call.

Generic deck gets built first. Niche-specific variants next. Personalization automation last. Tool layer is flexible: Gamma, Notebook LM, or Claude Design all work. Call transcript is the single most important input.

Why this closes Russ's gap: By the time prospects hit the sales team, they have seen a deck that uses their own business language to frame what COD actually delivers. They stop showing up cold.
03
Pobot used live on the triage call
Owner: Akari + Franzi

During the 15-minute triage, Franzi types the prospect's "I help [audience] with [outcome]" into Russ's Pobot. Bot materializes a tangible program version on screen in real time. Prospect sees a concrete picture of what their high-ticket offer could look like instead of an abstract "we have done this with 6,000 coaches" pitch.

Open question whether to declare AI use on the call or pretend it is Franzi's analysis. Franzi raised the concern that brick-and-mortar prospects might react badly to "you used a bot" if it is visible.

Origin: Akari's idea at 63:00. Solves the "high-ticket is too abstract" problem that comes up repeatedly with therapist targets.
04
Resource page link in cold email plants remarketing pixel
Owner: Kas

Replace "happy to send testimonials" with a link to a COD resource or vault page tailored for cold prospects. Page gets a tracking pixel. Even if only a small percentage actually consume the content, every clicker gets cookied and we then retarget them across Google, Facebook, and Instagram for cents per impression.

Cold email cannot legally include a CTA link directly in the cold body (deliverability and FTC). The link goes in the reply once the prospect engages. Optional: invisible pixel redirect in front of the Gamma deck URL from layer 2, which plants the cookie automatically as part of the existing journey.

Why this matters: Cold prospects will almost never commit to watching a 2.5-hour webinar. Plant the cookie, then let paid retargeting do the indoctrination work that the original Facebook ad sequence used to do.
05

Current coach-side cold campaign is not converting. Coaches already know what a high-ticket offer is, so the soft "we can show you the high-ticket model" pitch reads as either obvious or condescending. Pivot the coach campaign to pitching MA directly.

Therapist side keeps the indoctrination flow above. They genuinely need the model explained before any sales conversation makes sense.
