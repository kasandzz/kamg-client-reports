Attribution Clarity: Why the Numbers Differ

Critical Context
Three different systems report three different revenue numbers. This is expected and correct. Understanding why is essential before making any budget decisions.
Source Revenue Conversions Avg Revenue What It Tracks
Stripe (Ground Truth) $8,285 209 $39.64 $27 tickets + VIP upgrades only. Does NOT capture HT enrollments.
Meta (Platform-Reported) $125,336 1,264 $99.16 Modeled conversions. Over-counts via broad attribution, view-through, and probabilistic modeling.
Hyros (Server-Side) $133,948 236 $567.58 Full customer journey: ad click through $27 ticket through HT enrollment ($3K-$9K+). First-party server-side tracking.
Key Why Stripe Shows $8K and Hyros Shows $134K

This is not a discrepancy. These systems track different things:

  • Stripe query captures $27 workshop tickets and some VIP upgrades ($27+$27=$54). The AOV of $39.64 confirms this: mostly $27 tickets with some $54 VIPs mixed in.
  • Hyros tracks the full customer journey from ad click through workshop ticket through high-ticket enrollment. When someone buys a $27 ticket on March 25 and enrolls in COD Foundation ($8,452 PIF) on April 1, Hyros attributes that enrollment revenue back to the original ad click.
  • Meta over-counts conversions (1,264 vs Hyros's 236) because it uses 7-day click / 1-day view windows with probabilistic modeling. Many of those 1,264 "conversions" are modeled, not confirmed.
Decision Framework
Use Hyros for revenue attribution and ROAS decisions. Use Stripe to validate ticket volume and payment health. Use Meta only for relative campaign comparison (not absolute numbers).
Flag GHL Match Rate at 58.3% -- Attribution Gap Risk
Observation
Only 58.3% of Hyros leads match to GHL contacts. The remaining 41.7% are either: (a) entering GHL under different emails, (b) not making it into GHL at all, or (c) using different contact identifiers (phone vs email).
Hypothesis
The most likely cause is email mismatch between Meta lead capture (which may use Facebook profile email) and GHL (which uses form-submitted email). Second most likely: contacts entering via direct Stripe payment without passing through GHL form.
Recommendation
P1: Audit 50 random Hyros leads that fail GHL match. Check if they exist in GHL under different emails or phone numbers. If pattern emerges, add phone-based matching as secondary join key.

Insight Volume vs. value trade-off is stark

The data reveals two distinct cohort types:

  • High-ticket producers (Attorney $1,832 AOV, Coaches $756, Educator $728): Lower lead volume, lower conversion rate, but massive revenue per conversion. These drive the business.
  • Ticket-only cohorts (Therapists $225 AOV, Health Coaches $27 AOV): Higher lead volume, higher ticket conversion rate, but minimal high-ticket enrollment. These fill the workshop but don't pay the bills.

This means optimizing for lowest CPA is the wrong strategy. A campaign producing 10 attorney leads at $200 CPA is worth more than a campaign producing 100 health coach leads at $50 CPA.

Recommendation
Shift optimization from CPA (cost per ticket) to LTV-weighted ROAS. Build Hyros revenue segments into Meta as custom conversions so the algorithm optimizes for revenue, not just conversion events.

What's Working

Proven Winners

  • Attorney Only (8.36x ROAS) -- The #1 ad set by a massive margin. $4.4K spend returned $36.8K. Attorneys convert at $1,832 AOV, suggesting high-ticket enrollment is their default path.
  • Educator 3064 (6.90x ROAS) -- The volume play. $5.4K spend, 142 conversions, $37K revenue. Best combination of volume + revenue quality across the entire account.
  • Interest Stack Coaches 3064 Copy (2.20x ROAS) -- The reliable middle. $9K spend, $19.9K revenue. Not flashy but consistently profitable. 85.7% lead-to-conversion rate in Hyros is exceptional.
  • CAPI tracking is working -- 236 tracked sales with clear source attribution. This 12-day window provides the first reliable dataset COD has ever had for Meta ad optimization.
  • The ABO structure works -- Industry-specific ad sets allow precise performance isolation. Without ABO, the attorney signal would be buried inside a CBO average.

Structural Issues

  • 11 of ~17 ad sets are unprofitable. Only 3 ad sets generate real revenue. The rest consume budget and produce tickets that never convert to HT enrollment.
  • Retargeting is non-functional. $4.9K spent across two retargeting campaigns with $108 total revenue. Either the audiences are wrong, the creative is wrong, or attribution is misaligned.
  • Ad set duplication destroys value. Attorney Only = 8.36x. Its copies = 0.24-0.28x. Educator 3064 = 6.90x. Its age variant = 0.25x. Copies compete in the same auction and cannibalize the original.
  • GHL match rate at 58.3% means 41.7% of leads are falling through the tracking cracks. Full-journey visibility requires 80%+ match rate.
  • Therapist vertical is a volume trap. 20% of budget, highest conversion volume (316), but near-zero HT enrollment. Filling workshops but not the enrollment pipeline.
