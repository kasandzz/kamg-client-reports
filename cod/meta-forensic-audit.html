Structural Diagnostics

P0 No Cost Caps or Bid Caps -- Anywhere

Every single active campaign runs LOWEST_COST_WITHOUT_CAP. There are zero cost caps, zero bid caps, zero ROAS floors configured anywhere in either account.

Piliero M3 Method -- Cost Controls: "99% of advertisers spend uniformly. Cost caps and bid caps are the most important tools for preventing CPA creep. Without them, Meta will spend your daily budget regardless of how expensive each conversion becomes."

At $7K/day average spend, even a 10% CPA improvement from cost caps = $700/day = $21K/month savings.

Immediate Action
Implement cost caps on top 5 ad sets at 120% of their current CPA. Monitor delivery for 3 days. If delivery drops more than 30%, raise cap by $25 and retest. This is the single highest-leverage change available.
P0 Campaign Proliferation: 147 Campaigns Fragmenting Signal

Account #5 has 147 campaigns (5 active, 142 paused). Even the 5 active campaigns include 19 ad sets. Post-Andromeda best practice (per Piliero and Andromeda documentation) is 3-5 campaigns max:

  • Prospecting CBO -- cold audiences, all concepts
  • ASC (Advantage+) -- scaling layer for proven winners
  • Retention/Retargeting -- warm audiences only

The current ABO-dominant structure with industry-specific ad sets was appropriate pre-Andromeda. Post-Andromeda, the algorithm performs better with fewer campaigns, more ads per ad set, and broader targeting.

Test T1
Run a parallel CBO campaign with the top 5 winning creatives from current ABO ad sets. Same total budget. If CBO matches ABO performance within 14 days, begin migration.
P0 ABO vs CBO: Account is Pre-Andromeda Architecture

The primary spending campaign ($385K) is ABO. The industry niche campaign ($255K) is ABO. Only the therapist campaign ($30K) uses CBO. This is a pre-Andromeda structure that manually controls budget at the ad set level.

Post-Andromeda reality: Meta's algorithm now handles budget allocation better than manual ABO in most cases. CBO allows the algorithm to shift budget to winning ad sets within a campaign automatically -- which is exactly what this account needs (budget flowing to winners, not sitting on underperformers).

Key caveat: The niche ad sets (Attorney, Educator, Therapist) benefit from ABO because they have fundamentally different audiences. A CBO campaign would over-index on whichever niche gets cheap clicks, potentially starving high-LTV niches (Attorneys) in favor of high-CTR niches (Therapists).

Recommendation
Hybrid approach: CBO for broad targeting campaigns, ABO for niche-specific campaigns. Test T1 will validate which is better for COD's specific funnel.
P1 Creative Refresh Cadence Below Piliero Standard

Piliero standard: 1 new ad per $10K/week in spend. At $50K/week, COD should produce 5 new creatives per week.

Pedro's actual velocity: 203 ads created over the data window. That's roughly 6/week -- slightly above minimum. However, "new ads" includes duplicates, copies, and minor variations. True concept diversity (Andromeda requirement: 80% new concepts, 20% variations) is likely much lower.

Andromeda impact: The algorithm now groups similar creatives together. If all ads are variations of the same "Russ talking to camera" format, Meta treats them as one creative signal. You need format diversity: UGC, before/after, PAS, carousel, demonstration, AI-generated.

P1 Three Management Transitions with No Strategy Continuity
