4 Key Findings

1. Healthcare Providers is a Hidden 6th Segment

Pedro's "Health Coaches" only covers "Health & Wellness Coaches" ad sets ($8,605 spend, 22 purchases). But COD also runs separate ad sets for "Physician/DO/NP" and "Other Licensed Healthcare Provider" (Acupuncturists, Chiropractors, PTs, Dietitians). These generated 39 Hyros purchases and have the highest show rate (95.2%) but aren't tracked in Pedro's table.

RECOMMENDATION: Break out as separate row with its own spend allocation from Facebook Ads Manager.

2. Hyros API Data Gap

Pedro's "Purchases" (885 total) come from Hyros scientific attribution. Our API pull only got 628 sales because CAPI was installed Feb 22 -- missing ~3 weeks of February data. The ratios between segments are consistent across both data sets, confirming the directional accuracy.

GAP: ~257 sales between Pedro (885) and API (628). Ratio consistency validates segment proportions.

3. Show Rates are Strong Across the Board

Hyros call data shows 87-91% show rates for the four main segments. Only Health Coaches is low (60%) but that's a 5-call sample -- statistically meaningless. Coaches & Consultants lead at 91.2%. Healthcare Providers top all segments at 95.2%.

NOTE: Show rate = Hyros QUALIFIED state / total calls. This tracks people who connected with a closer, not just attended.

4. Educators Have the Best Enrollment Rate

Despite smallest volume (6 enrollments from 21 booked calls), Educators convert at the highest rate and have the lowest CPA ($4,337). This is 25% cheaper than Therapists ($5,770) despite Therapists being the largest segment by spend and volume.

OPPORTUNITY: Educators may be underinvested. Current spend is only 10% of total ($26K of $249K).

5. 52% of Bookings are Unattributed

412 of 809 booking events have no Facebook ad tracking. These are mostly rescheduled entries (which lose UTM data) and organic/internal bookings. The BCT cross-reference recovered 352 of 697 BCT rows (50.5%) through UTM, workshop email, and GHL lookups.

IMPACT: Attribution leakage inflates unattributed pool. Recommend UTM persistence fix in GHL booking forms.

6 Caveats
  • Hyros data starts Feb 22 (CAPI install date) -- missing approximately 3 weeks of February. Pedro's numbers cover the full range via Hyros UI.
  • BCT enrollment counts are from Feb + Mar only. April data is not yet reflected in the BCT tabs.
  • "Enrollments" = BCT rows where Sentiment = "Enrollment" -- this means they enrolled on the call, not necessarily that payment has been collected.
  • Show rates from Hyros (QUALIFIED state) vs BCT (call completed) may differ slightly due to different tracking criteria.
  • 17 enrollments remain unattributed across all sources -- could not be classified to any segment via UTM, workshop, or GHL methods.
  • Health Coaches sample size is very small (5 calls, 2 enrollments) -- treat all Health Coaches metrics as directional only.
  • Conversion lag: recent leads (late March / early April) haven't had time to convert through the full funnel, so current-month CPA will improve as sales close.
7 Methodology

Segment attribution was determined through a 5-method cascade. Each booking was classified by the first method that produced a match:

UTM Medium Keyword Matching
Parsed utm_medium from booking URLs. Matched keywords like "therapist", "attorney", "coach" to segments. 154 bookings attributed.
Landing Page URL Path Matching
Matched booking source URLs against known segment-specific landing pages (e.g., /therapist-workshop, /attorney-masterclass). 48 bookings attributed.
Workshop Email Cross-Reference
Joined booking contact emails against workshop registration lists that are segment-tagged. 185 bookings attributed.
GHL CRM Contact Lookup
For remaining unattributed contacts, queried GoHighLevel CRM for contact tags and ad source fields. 10 bookings attributed.
Hyros Ad Source Name Classification
Classified Hyros call and sale records by parsing the ad source/campaign name field. 321 of 531 calls, 484 of 628 sales classified.
