PostHog Funnel Intelligence

What It Is, What It Does, and How to Install It

Executive Summary

We are upgrading PostHog from basic pageview tracking to full funnel intelligence. This means we will finally be able to see exactly where buyers drop off between registration and enrollment, which pages and audiences convert best, and where the biggest revenue leaks are hiding. Installation takes about 15 minutes and requires pasting 6 code snippets into GHL page footers.

What PostHog Tracks Today vs. What It Will Track

Right Now (Basic)

PostHog currently tracks simple pageviews. It tells us that someone visited a page. That is it. No context about who they are, what they bought, which workshop they attended, or whether they booked a call.

After Installation (Full Funnel Intelligence)

PostHog will track the entire buyer journey with rich context at every stage:

Funnel Stage What Gets Tracked
Registration Page visited, source, device, timestamp
Ticket Purchase ($27) Purchase completed, buyer identified, VIP vs Standard
Workshop Attendance Whether the buyer actually showed up and watched
Call Booking Booking completed, time between purchase and booking
Enrollment Final conversion from prospect to paying client

Every event carries context. Not just "someone visited" but "this person who bought a $27 ticket from this ad set on mobile watched 80% of the workshop and booked a call within 2 hours."

How Installation Works

This is not a complex technical project. Here is what is involved:

  • What: 6 JavaScript code snippets, one for each key funnel page
  • Where: Each snippet gets pasted into the footer code section of its corresponding GHL funnel page
  • Who: Ryan (or anyone with GHL funnel editor access)
  • Time: Approximately 15 minutes total
  • Skill level: Copy and paste. No coding knowledge required.

Each snippet is clearly labeled with which page it belongs to. Open the GHL page editor, scroll to the footer/tracking code section, paste the snippet, save. Repeat for all 6 pages.

What Changes for You

For customers and prospects: absolutely nothing. This tracking is completely invisible. No pop-ups, no speed changes, no visual differences. The buyer experience stays exactly the same.

For the team: You gain access to PostHog dashboards that show real-time funnel performance. No new tools to learn day-to-day. The data just starts flowing, and we build dashboards and reports on top of it.

What This Means for Revenue

COD's funnel has 4 identified leak zones. Every one of them becomes measurable and fixable with proper tracking in place.

Leak 1: Workshop Show Rate (currently 40-65%)

Right now, somewhere between 35% and 60% of people who buy a $27 ticket never watch the workshop. That is potentially half of all ad spend wasted before the sales process even begins. With PostHog tracking, we will see show rates in real time, identify which traffic sources produce the best attendees, and measure whether our pre-workshop reminder sequences are working.

Leak 2: Ticket-to-Booking (75-84% never book a call)

This is the single biggest revenue killer. Out of every 100 ticket buyers, roughly 75 to 84 never book a sales call. Even among VIP buyers who watch the entire workshop, many disappear without booking. Tracking lets us identify exactly where in the post-workshop flow people drop off and test interventions to recover them.

Leak 3: No-Shows and Cancellations

No-shows have been increasing month over month. At roughly $877 per booked call, February's 57 no-shows represented approximately $50,000 in wasted ad spend. With full journey tracking, we can identify patterns in who no-shows (day of week, time gap between booking and call, traffic source) and build targeted prevention sequences.

Leak 4: Close Rate Optimization

The current enrollment rate from sales calls varies dramatically by closer, audience, and funnel path. Tracking the full journey means we can see which combination of registration page, ticket type, workshop attendance pattern, and booking timing produces the highest close rates. This lets us route the best leads to the best closers and optimize every step upstream.

The Stacking Effect

None of these leaks exist in isolation. Improving show rate from 42% to 55%, booking rate from 20% to 30%, show-up rate from 75% to 85%, and close rate from 14% to 18% does not add up. It multiplies. Those four modest improvements together can push ROAS from 1.7x toward 3x on the same ad spend.

But you cannot improve what you cannot measure. That is what this installation unlocks.

What Patterns We Can Find (Examples)

This is not just conversion tracking. Once we layer PostHog behavioral data with Hyros attribution, Stripe revenue, and GHL contact data in the warehouse, we can start answering questions that most $10M+ companies still guess at.

Which Parts of the Landing Page People Actually Read

PostHog records heatmaps and session recordings on every page automatically. This means we can see:

  • Scroll depth by buyer vs non-buyer. Do people who purchase the $27 ticket scroll past the testimonials? Do they read the FAQ? Or do they stop at the value stack and buy immediately? If 80% of buyers never scroll past Section 6, everything below it is wasted real estate.
  • What people click on that is not a button. Dead clicks reveal what visitors expect to be interactive. If 200 people per week click on "Russ has helped 6,000+ clients" expecting more detail, that is a signal to make it a link or expand the proof.
  • Rage clicks and u-turns. When someone clicks the same spot 5 times and then leaves, PostHog flags it. This catches broken elements, confusing layouts, and friction points invisible to analytics.
  • Section-by-section attention. PostHog shows exactly how long visitors pause on each section. If the "Is This You?" pain-point section holds attention for 12 seconds but the "Before/After" table gets skipped in 2 seconds, that tells us which emotional triggers are landing and which are not.

A VWO study across 5,000+ experiments found that heatmap-informed landing page redesigns improve conversion rates by 20-30% on average. For COD at $27 per ticket and ~$200 cost per purchase, even a 15% lift in registration-to-purchase means thousands per month in recovered revenue.

The Exact Psychological Profile of Everybody Who Spends Money

Once PostHog identifies users by email and we join that with Hyros attribution and GHL contact data in BigQuery, we can build a behavioral fingerprint of every buyer. Not demographics. Behavior.

What the buyer profile reveals:

  • How they found us. Not just "Facebook ad." The exact ad set, creative, audience segment, and landing page variant they came through. We can see if therapists who arrive via the "Licensed Therapists CBO" ad set convert 3x better than those from broad targeting.
  • What they did before buying. Did they visit 3 times before purchasing? Did they spend 4 minutes reading testimonials? Did they watch the hero video? PostHog tracks every session leading up to conversion.
  • How fast they move. Buyers who purchase within 60 seconds of landing behave differently from those who come back the next day. Speed-to-purchase is a strong signal of buyer temperature.
  • What content they consume. Workshop watch duration, which segments they rewatch, where they pause or leave. Someone who watches 120 minutes and then books is a fundamentally different prospect than someone who watches 20 minutes and bounces.
  • Where they hesitate. If a group of buyers all pause on the pricing section for 30+ seconds, that is price sensitivity. If they all skip the "Not For You" section, they already self-selected.

The practical profile looks like this:

"Your highest-value buyers (those who enroll at $8K+) share these traits: they arrive from profession-specific ad sets (not broad), they read the full landing page (scroll depth 85%+), they purchase within the first visit, they watch 90+ minutes of the workshop, and they book a call within 4 hours of finishing. They are most likely to arrive on Tuesday or Wednesday between 10am and 2pm."

That profile is not a guess. It is built from actual behavioral data across hundreds of buyers.

How We Apply This Data Practically

This is where it gets powerful. Behavioral profiles are not just interesting. They drive concrete decisions.

Ads optimization. When we know that buyers who arrive from the "Licensed Therapists CBO" ad set have 3.5x the enrollment rate of broad targeting, we reallocate budget. Facebook's own research (Meta Business Help Center, 2024) shows that advertisers who feed conversion data back into their targeting see 20-35% improvement in cost per acquisition. PostHog-identified users, matched to Hyros sale data, give us the exact conversion signal to optimize around.

Landing page personalization. If session recordings show that attorneys spend 2x longer on the credibility section but skip the "Is This You?" section entirely (because they already know their problem), we can build attorney-specific pages that lead with Russ's credentials and cut the agitation copy. Unbounce's 2023 Conversion Benchmark Report found that pages matched to visitor intent convert 2-4x better than generic pages.

Workshop timing and format. If watch-duration data shows that 60% of non-bookers drop off at the 45-minute mark (right before the offer reveal), we know the workshop needs a re-engagement hook at minute 40. Wistia's research across 100M+ video sessions shows that engagement drops 20% at the 2-minute mark and another 30% at the halfway point. Knowing your exact drop-off curve lets you place hooks precisely.

Sales routing. When we can see the full journey of every person who books a call, we can score them before the call happens. Someone who bought VIP, watched 120 minutes, and booked same-day is a hot lead. Route them to Dorian (25% close rate). Someone who bought standard, watched 20 minutes, and booked 5 days later needs a different approach. Route them to a discovery call first. HubSpot's 2024 Sales Enablement Report found that lead scoring based on behavioral data increases close rates by 30% and reduces sales cycle length by 18%.

Retargeting. PostHog can feed audience segments directly to Meta. People who watched 90+ minutes of the workshop but did not book a call are the warmest possible retargeting audience. They already trust Russ, they already understand the offer. They just need a nudge. AdRoll's cross-industry data shows retargeting converts 70% higher than cold prospecting, but only when the audience is precisely defined. Right now COD has no retargeting based on workshop behavior because that data does not exist yet. It will after this install.

Email/SMS sequences. When we know that the average time between ticket purchase and workshop attendance is 36 hours but same-day attendees convert 2x better, every reminder sequence can be calibrated to compress that gap. The pre-workshop SMS at 2 hours, 30 minutes, and 5 minutes before showtime becomes data-driven instead of guesswork.
