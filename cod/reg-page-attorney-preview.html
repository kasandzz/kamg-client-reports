Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

"I'm a licensed attorney. Are there bar ethics concerns with offering coaching or consulting?" +
Coaching and consulting are distinct from practicing law. You're not providing legal advice, forming an attorney-client relationship, or representing anyone in a legal matter. You're packaging your expertise into an educational and advisory program focused on outcomes -- not legal representation. That said, we always recommend reviewing your state bar's rules on outside business activities, advertising, and fee arrangements. Many attorneys already offer consulting and advisory services under existing rules.
"Is this the unauthorized practice of law (UPL)?" +
No. A consulting or coaching program is explicitly NOT legal practice. You're teaching frameworks, sharing expertise, and guiding clients toward outcomes -- not drafting documents, appearing in court, or rendering legal opinions. The line is clear: if it's education and strategy, it's consulting. If it's legal advice for a specific matter, it's law. This workshop helps you build the former.
"What exactly happens in the workshop?" +
It's a 3-hour working session, not a lecture. Russ walks you through his offer-building framework, you use AI tools to map out your consulting program in real time, and he reviews attendee offers live. You walk away with a mission statement, an 8-week program outline, and lesson plans you can use immediately.
"I don't want to stop practicing law. Do I have to?" +
Not at all. Many of our members run their consulting program alongside their existing practice. Some transition fully over time. Some keep both indefinitely. The consulting program gives you OPTIONS and additional revenue streams -- that's the point.
"Will this work for my specific practice area?" +
If your expertise helps people solve a meaningful problem -- employment disputes, estate planning, business formation, compliance, contracts, family law, real estate -- then yes. The framework is practice-area-agnostic. We've had attorneys, CPAs, financial advisors, and other licensed professionals all build successful offers from their domain expertise.
"Is this just another upsell funnel?" +
The workshop itself is pure content. Russ's house rule: no bait-and-switch. You'll learn his complete framework in 3 hours. If you want to go deeper afterward, there's an option to apply for his coaching program -- but the workshop stands alone as a complete, actionable experience.
"$27 seems too cheap. What's the catch?" +
There's no catch. We charge $27 because it filters for serious professionals. Free webinars attract tire-kickers. When you pay, you pay attention. This workshop has produced the highest-quality leads in our 13-year history -- because the people who show up are ready to work.
"I'm already making good money. Why would I do this?" +
The question isn't whether you're making money. It's whether you're making $10,000-$20,000 per consulting client instead of $300-$500 per hour. Whether you're working 15 hours a week instead of 55. The professionals in the $100K-$1M range are actually the most underserved -- they're earning well but they're stuck on the treadmill. This gives you the exit.
