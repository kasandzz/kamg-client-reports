2-Step Modal Checkout: Build & Split Test

COD $27 Workshop — Convert full-page redirect into in-page modal with email-capture step
From: Kas To: Rehan Date: 2026-05-12 Priority: HIGH
Show me a Loom of the full modal flow on desktop AND mobile before activating the PostHog experiment. Same rule as always — nothing live without my eyes on it first.
TL;DR — what we're doing and why: The current $27 workshop checkout sends people away from the reg page to a full GHL checkout page. We lose two things: (1) the context of the reg page, and (2) any data on people who bail before paying. We're replacing that with a modal that opens on top of the reg page. Step 1 asks for name + email and saves to GHL immediately. Step 2 collects payment. Even if they bail at Step 2, we have their email and can run an abandoned-checkout sequence. We'll test it 50/50 against the current flow using a PostHog feature flag so we have real numbers before we kill the old one.
1
The Big Picture (read this first)

Current flow (what users see today)

Reg page → Click "Save my seat" → Redirected to GHL checkout page → Pay $27

The problem: If they don't complete payment, we have nothing on them. No email, no name, no way to follow up. Lots of money walks out the door silently.

New flow (what we're building)

Reg page → Click "Save my seat" → Modal opens (Step 1: Name + Email) → ✓ Saved to GHL → Step 2 (Payment) → Pay $27

The win: Even if they bail at Step 2, we already have their name + email saved as a GHL contact tagged workshop-checkout-started. That triggers a 3-email abandoned-checkout sequence to bring them back. Free pipeline from people who would have ghosted us.

Why we're doing this
$27 workshop is the top of the COD funnel — every checkout abandon is a lost call/enrollment downstream. Even a 5% recovery rate from abandoned-checkout emails is huge over a month. We also want cleaner attribution: with Step 1 captured, we can match GHL contacts back to PostHog sessions and ad campaigns.

Set Up the PostHog Experiment (50/50 split)
Platform — PostHog
app.posthog.com → COD project → Experiments → New experiment

Why PostHog, not GHL A/B

GHL's built-in A/B is too dumb — it can't do proper stat significance, it can't segment by traffic source, and we can't cross-reference with ad spend. PostHog handles the split, the events, and the math.

Experiment setup

Must Do

Create the feature flag

  • Feature flag key: checkout-modal-2step-v1
  • Variants: control (50%) and modal-2step (50%)
  • Rollout: 100% of users on the $27 reg page get bucketed
  • Persistence: by distinct_id (so the same visitor always sees the same variant, even across sessions)
Must Do

Wire it into the reg page

Pseudo-code for what the CTA click needs to do:

// On reg page load
posthog.onFeatureFlags(function() {
  const variant = posthog.getFeatureFlag('checkout-modal-2step-v1');
  // variant is 'control' or 'modal-2step'

  document.querySelectorAll('.cta-save-seat').forEach(btn => {
    btn.addEventListener('click', function(e) {
      if (variant === 'modal-2step') {
        e.preventDefault();
        openCheckoutModal();   // new flow
      }
      // else: let the existing link redirect (control flow)
    });
  });
});

Make sure the PostHog snippet loads BEFORE any CTA can be clicked, otherwise the variant won't be known yet. If you're unsure how, ping me — we have a working pattern from the prior PostHog setup.

Primary metric (what wins the test)

Must Do

Conversion rate from CTA click → paid

  • Goal event: checkout_completed
  • Entry event: checkout_cta_clicked
  • Minimum sample: 1,000 CTA-clicks per variant OR 14 days, whichever comes first
  • Significance threshold: 95% confidence (PostHog default)
Explore (secondary metrics)

What we also want to see in the dashboard

  • Step-1 capture rate (modal variant only): % of CTA-clicks that submit Step 1
  • Step-1 → Step-2 advance rate
  • Step-2 → paid rate (the "new abandon rate")
  • Time on page before CTA click (does the modal change browsing behaviour?)
  • Mobile vs desktop split for each variant
