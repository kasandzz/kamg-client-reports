The Submit Button on B is Broken

B's content is working better — the engagement metrics prove it. But there's a critical submit-button bug on B that needs fixing before you can draw any fair conclusions about conversion rates.

! Root Cause: What's Actually Happening

Every single CTA on the B page — "Save My Seat Now," "Save My Seat – Just $27," "Get VIP Access" — are all anchor links pointing to:

href="#section-K5FyHwqhu6"

They scroll the user down to the form section. They are page navigation links, not submission buttons.

The actual pay/submit button inside the form has zero clicks captured. PostHog has not recorded a single click on any checkout form submission element on the B page.

What session recordings showed

Users clicked "Save My Seat Now," the page scrolled to #section-K5FyHwqhu6, but the form section was either empty, broken, or invisible — so they saw nothing and left. Multiple users clicked a second time and then abandoned.

? Most Likely Cause

The form inside #section-K5FyHwqhu6 is broken or not loading. The CTAs scroll the user to that section, but the actual checkout form either:

  • Isn't rendering — the section loads blank or shows nothing interactive
  • Is inside a misconfigured iframe — GHL embeds checkout forms in iframes, and the B page's form widget may not have been reconnected when the page was duplicated
  • Has a JavaScript error blocking it — multiple B sessions recorded console errors that could be killing the form before it renders

✓ Fix Checklist

B variant — fix immediately

1
Open the B page and scroll to #section-K5FyHwqhu6. Does the form actually appear?
2
Check browser console for JavaScript errors. Session data shows console errors on multiple B sessions.
3
Compare the HTML of section K5FyHwqhu6 between A and B. On A it renders a working form. On B it may be misconfigured, pointing to the wrong form ID, or missing entirely.
4
In GHL page builder, reconnect the form widget. If B was duplicated from A, the embedded order form widget may not have been properly reconnected to the B variant.
5
Run a test conversion on B before resuming traffic.

Both variants — fix regardless of winner

1
Date picker: Rage clicks on the date selector appear on both variants. It's a shared component blocking conversions at the very first form interaction.
2
Payment checkbox (A): Replace with a clearer consent pattern (inline text confirmation or more obvious toggle).
3
Phone field (A): Audit the input mask. Consider removing it or switching to a simpler number-only input.

Bottom Line

B variant shows better engagement metrics (longer sessions, lower bounce, fewer rage clicks) and its content is likely stronger. However, the unresponsive submit button is a critical technical blocker that is artificially suppressing B's conversion rate. Fix the submit button bug on B before drawing any conclusions about which variant converts better.
