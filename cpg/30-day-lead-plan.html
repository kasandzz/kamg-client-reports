The 6 Moves

Every move patches a leak or opens a new channel. In order of speed-to-revenue.

1
Week 1 Start
Upgrade Steven's Cold Email Engine
Right now only ~10% of outbound is personalized. Email servers catch templates. Clay + Claude makes every email 100% unique, written specifically for the prospect. Steven stays, he just gets a better weapon.
  • Set up Clay enrichment for prospect data (company, role, recent news)
  • Build Claude prompt chain: Clay data in, personalized email out
  • Train on top-performing VALERIA-era campaigns (we have the data)
  • Steven tests on 500 prospects, measure reply rate vs. current
  • If it works: scale to all campaigns within 2 weeks
Clay: ~$150/mo
Claude API: ~$50/mo
Setup: 3-4 days
2
Week 1 Start
Build New List Segments
63% of volume comes from one campaign (International Stores). If that goes cold, everything dies. We need 3-4 new segments based on which verticals actually close.
  • Analyze pipeline: which verticals closed at highest rate?
  • Extract 3-4 new ICP segments from closed-won patterns
  • Build prospect lists via Apollo/Clay (1,000-2,000 per segment)
  • Launch new campaigns in Instantly with personalized copy
  • Run for 2 weeks, measure, double down on winners
Apollo/Clay: ~$200/mo
New domains: ~$100
Setup: 2-3 days
3
Week 1-2
Hire a Competing Agency to Benchmark
One email operator is a single point of failure. A second agency running in parallel gives us a benchmark: if they outperform Steven's setup, we know the ceiling. If Steven wins, we know we have the right guy.
  • Source 2-3 cold email agencies with affiliate/DTC experience
  • Share ICP + current metrics as brief (we have everything)
  • Run parallel for 30 days on separate domains
  • Compare: reply rate, opportunity rate, cost per opp
  • Decision at 30 days: scale winner, cut loser
Agency retainer: $2-5K/mo
Domains: ~$100
Lead time: 1 week to start
4
Week 2-3
Run More List Tests for Krik
Beyond segments, we need to rapidly test messaging angles, subject lines, CTAs, and send times. Right now there's no A/B testing culture. Every variable should be tested within 30 days.
  • Set up systematic A/B tests in Instantly: subject lines, opening hooks, CTAs
  • Test softer CTAs vs. direct ask (Steven already started this)
  • Test send times: morning vs. afternoon vs. evening by timezone
  • Test ICP targeting: company size, revenue range, role title variations
  • Weekly report to Krik: what won, what lost, what's next
Cost: $0 (existing tools)
Kas time: ~4 hrs/week
Results: 14-day cycles
5
Week 2-3
Fix Julieta's Sales Process + AI Agent
Julieta handles every inbound reply by hand. No CRM. Leads go missing. She wants to close more deals but she's buried in admin. An AI agent drafts her replies, qualifies leads, and surfaces follow-ups she'd otherwise miss.
  • Get Fathom access: analyze Krik's winning close patterns
  • Collect Julieta's reply templates + sales emails as training data
  • Build Claude agent: auto-drafts qualification replies, suggests follow-up timing
  • Implement simple CRM tracking (even a shared Google Sheet to start)
  • Restructure: Julieta takes discovery/warm-up calls, Krik closes big deals only
Claude API: ~$30/mo
CRM (basic): $0-50/mo
Build time: 5-7 days
6
Week 3-4
Landing Page + Google Ads Channel
Cold email is the only scaled channel. Google Ads opens a completely new pipeline -- inbound leads who are actively searching for affiliate partnerships. Daniel Hall is already building a $2K test campaign. We just need the landing page.
  • Build a focused landing page: "CPG Affiliate Partners" -- what you do, proof, CTA
  • Case studies from existing wins (Beat Basta, top clients)
  • Google Ads campaign targeting: "affiliate marketing agency", "CPG affiliate program", etc.
  • Daniel Hall manages the ads, Kas builds the page
  • Track: cost per lead, cost per booked call, cost per close
Ad spend: $2K/mo (already approved)
LP build: 2-3 days
