The Philosophy (Read This First)

Mindset

This is not a dial-and-pray operation. The reason this system works is because it is built on genuine human connection in an era where every brand operator is drowning in automation and AI outreach.

Your setter is not "just a setter." They are the front door to CPG. They are the first real human voice your prospect hears. If that experience is cold, scripted, or robotic, the lead is already lost, and a brand operator who could have been a six-figure partnership walks away.

In a benchmark deployment of this exact system, 60% of all enrolments were set by an appointment setter, not self-booked, not automated. Real humans, doing the work, with genuine curiosity. For CPG that maps to a partnership pipeline that compounds, brands that stay for 18+ months, and a sales motion that scales without burning founders out.

Core mindset: Be obsessed with the affiliate model, genuinely curious about the brand's growth story, and treat every conversation like it matters. Because it does.
01

Setter Mindset & Readiness

Foundation

Before your setter touches a phone or opens Attio, they need to internalise five principles.

1.1 Be obsessed with the offer

Your setter has to actually believe in the CPG affiliate model. They need to understand why performance-based beats fixed-fee for DTC brands, know the case studies by heart (which brands scaled, what the net-new-customer percentage looked like, how long it took), and genuinely get excited when a brand operator describes a problem CPG solves. If your setter doesn't believe the model is the best deal in the market, they cannot sell the next conversation.

1.2 Be genuinely curious

This is not fake curiosity scripting. When a brand founder tells you their AOV, their current CAC, or their channel mix, you should actually want to hear about it. Ask follow-up questions because you are interested in their business, not because a script told you to. People can feel the difference instantly, and DTC operators have been pitched by enough agencies to spot fake interest in three seconds.

1.3 Take care of yourself first

If you are stressed, tired, sick, dealing with family issues, or in a bad mood, do not get near prospects. Your energy transfers through the phone. Sleep, water, supplements, daily steps. This is a performance role and you need to show up performing.

1.4 You are not "just a setter"

The setter role is not an entry-level stepping stone to becoming a closer or a strategist. It is a specialised skill set that requires thick skin, quick wit, the ability to build instant rapport with brand founders, and genuine people skills. A great setter is the most valuable person on the CPG sales team. Own that identity.

1.5 Gamify everything

Track your bookings visually. Colour-code your Attio calendar (yellow for showed, green for closed-into-partnership). Compete with yourself. Beat Monday's number on Tuesday. Make the dopamine hit of a confirmed booking your fuel. This keeps energy high across full work days.

The Reply-First System

The Core Move

2.1 Why this exists

Cold calling is effectively dead for appointment setting. Modern iOS and Android updates mean calls from unknown numbers often do not even ring. The phone silently rejects them. The workaround is simple: text first, get permission to call, then call. Once a brand operator has texted with you, your number is no longer unknown. The phone rings. They pick up. They expect you.

This single shift, texting before calling, is the highest-leverage change you can make to your appointment-setting process. Data from connected calls run this way shows near-100% booking rates.

2.2 The four main lead sources (and how the trigger differs)

2.3 The initial message template (form-fill triggers)

Hey! [Setter Name] here from the CPG Affiliate Partners team. Saw you filled out the form on our site looking at the affiliate program. I'd love to grab 5 minutes with you to learn a bit about [Brand Name] so we can figure out whether you'd be a strong fit for the program. Won't take long. I'm free for the next hour. When's a good time to call?

Why each element earns its place

  • Uses the setter's first name, not the founder's name. You are a real person, not an automated blast.
  • References what they signed up for. Confirms they took an action, which validates the outreach.
  • Frames it as a fit check, not a pitch. "Figure out whether you'd be a strong fit" inverts the power dynamic, brands actually want to qualify for CPG, not be pitched to.
  • Creates a soft time constraint. "I'm free for the next hour" creates urgency without pressure.
  • Asks permission to call. "When's a good time to call?" hands them agency and ensures the phone rings.

2.4 The initial reply template (cold email / inbound)

When a brand operator replies to a Bison or Instantly cold email, your job is to keep the energy up and route them to a fast conversation. Lead with the case study or audit hook, then trigger the booking.

Hey [First Name], thanks for getting back to me. Quick question before I send anything over: what's your current customer acquisition setup like, mostly Meta, Google, organic, or some mix? And is there a particular vertical or AOV range I should pull case studies from?

Happy to drop you a one-pager from a brand we'd consider a close cousin to yours, and then we can grab 5 minutes to walk through whether the model would actually move the needle for [Brand Name]. What's the best mobile number to text you so we can lock in a time?

Important nuance for case-study hooks: Until they have spoken to you, send only a one-page anonymised case study or a vertical-specific benchmark, not your full deck or onboarding pack. A one-pager takes minutes to assemble in Notion or Google Docs and creates the same trust signal as the full deck. Save the full deck for prospects who have booked or proven they are real fit.

2.5 A note on the setter's name

If your setter has a name that is hard to pronounce or might cause confusion for DTC brand operators (most are US-based), consider using a shortened or anglicised version. Not a fake name. Something close to their real name that flows easily in conversation. Zero friction. If a brand founder has to ask "what was your name again?" you have already lost momentum.

Attio Setup (CPG-Specific)

Tooling

This SOP assumes Attio is your source of truth (which it is at CPG today). The same mechanics apply if you ever migrate, but the workflow names below map to Attio's stages and the Bison + GAds LP sync that Leo built.

3.1 For the GAds LP funnel (simplest setup)

This is the easiest version. A brand fills the form on thecpgaffiliate.com. The form sync writes a Lead in Attio. The text fires automatically. Your setter watches for replies.

  • Trigger: New Lead in Attio with source = gads_lp AND icp_label = qualified
  • Action: Send SMS using the form-fill template above
  • Tag added: [setter-name]-contacted
  • Smart list: Tag = setter-name AND has NOT booked a call

3.2 For Bison cold outbound (batch setup)

Compliance rule: Do NOT blast all replies at once. Carrier compliance (TCPA / 10DLC in the US) will flag your account if you send hundreds of texts simultaneously from the same number at the same time daily. Brand operators reading this kind of blast feel it and tune out.

The fix: work the Bison reply queue manually for the first 90 days, then add drip pacing.

  • Pull the day's Bison replies marked interested in Attio
  • Reply in-thread within 15 minutes of the reply landing
  • If volume forces a queue, pace at 15 to 25 contacts every 10 to 15 minutes
  • Example: 60 interested replies = roughly 30 to 40 minutes to clear

3.3 Staying organised in Attio

Tagging system

  • When you start a conversation with a brand, add a tag with your name (e.g. krik-setter or juli-setter)
  • Create a smart list that filters: Tag = your name AND has NOT booked a call
  • This becomes your active follow-up list, every brand you have contacted who has not booked yet
  • Once they book, the Lead → Deal promotion (W6 in Leo's build) tags them as call-scheduled and they fall off the list automatically

Phone notifications

Set up Attio's mobile app and turn on push notifications. You will often catch replies faster on your phone than watching the desktop conversations tab, especially in the first hour after the SMS fires.

Pro tip: If the Attio smart-list method feels too complex on day one, the old-school fallback works. Copy and paste every brand name that replies into a Google Sheet or note. Search them manually in Attio for follow-up. Not elegant. Fully functional.

The Phone Call Script

The Conversation

When a brand operator texts back "yeah, call me" or "I'm free now" or "give me 10 minutes", you call immediately. Speed matters enormously here. The motivation window is small, and DTC founders are interrupt-driven.

4.1 Opening (15 to 30 seconds)

Hey! This is [Setter Name] from the CPG team. How are you doing?

Say "how are you doing?" like you actually mean it. Slow it down slightly. This is a pattern interrupt. Most setters rush through it or sound robotic. When you say it with genuine warmth, it stops people in their tracks. They actually answer.

If they ask "how are you?"

Have a short, relatable, slightly funny human answer ready. Not "I'm good."

  • "I'm on my third coffee and my dog is going crazy under my desk, but having a great day. Thanks for asking."
  • "Living the dream over here, the Wi-Fi held up today so I'm calling that a win."

Then pivot: "But that's not why I'm calling..."

If they do NOT ask how you are

Have a seasonal or topical pivot ready:

  • "Happy New Year! Can I still say that?" (January to February)
  • "Hey, did your team survive Q4 / BFCM / back to school?"
  • "Happy Monday, we made it through another weekend!"
The point: Be a human being. Create a micro-moment of real connection before you get into the reason for the call. In a world of AI and automation, being a real, warm, slightly funny person is your unfair advantage. DTC founders deal with 30 agency pitches a month, none of them sound like a human.

4.2 The transition (10 seconds)

Alright, but that's not why I'm calling. I'm calling because I saw you [filled out the form on our site / replied to my email about the affiliate program]. I just want to make sure we'd actually be a fit before we waste anyone's time, so I've got a few quick questions and then I can put you in front of the right person on our side.

This frames you as a gatekeeper protecting both sides' time, not a salesperson. Brand founders respect that, because the agencies that get in their way are the ones that pitch before qualifying.

4.3 The discovery question (the golden moment)

While I have you, help me out real quick. Our team always wants to make sure we're matching the right brands to the right strategist. Do you mind sharing what's going on with the business right now that made the affiliate angle interesting?

This is where the magic happens. This single question opens the floodgates. High-quality brand operators will start telling you everything: their current channel mix, what's flatlining, what their AOV is, what they tried last quarter, who their growth lead is. You will not have to dig, they will volunteer.

What to listen for

  • What does the brand actually sell, and what's the AOV?
  • Who is their target customer? (Demo, vertical, repeat behaviour)
  • Are they already at scale or still finding their channels?
  • What's their current CAC, and what's the gap to LTV?
  • Do they already work with affiliates, influencers, or a partnership marketer?
  • How busy is the founder personally? (Tells you about decision-making speed)
  • Are they currently investing in growth, or in cost-cutting mode?

4.4 Digging deeper (conversational, not interrogation)

Use natural follow-ups. React genuinely to what they tell you.

  • "Oh interesting, tell me more about that."
  • "That's a great category, who's the avatar, is it mostly first-time buyers or repeat?"
  • "If everything went right with the program over the next six months, what would that look like for you?"
  • "Are you running paid right now, or mostly leaning on organic and email?"
  • "What would you say is the biggest bottleneck in the business right now?"
  • "Have you tried other affiliate or influencer programs before? What didn't work?"
The actor element: You have to genuinely make them feel like you are interested in their brand, their growth story, their customers. Most DTC founders work alone in their head all day. No one ever asks them the question they actually want to talk about. When you show up with real curiosity and enthusiasm for their business specifically, they light up. They feel seen.

If their brand is genuinely a fit, tell them: "Honestly that sounds like exactly the kind of brand our model was built for." Only say this if you mean it. Authenticity is non-negotiable. CPG's reputation in the DTC world depends on it.

If they are not a fit (too small, wrong vertical, services not products), pivot to the hypothetical: "Okay, so hypothetically, if we got the customer-acquisition piece dialled in, what would that unlock for the business?" Then route them honestly. If it's a no, say it on the call.

4.5 The transition to booking (the insider frame)

After the discovery conversation, transition to scheduling the strategist call.

Based on what you just told me, [Brand Name] sounds like a strong fit for the program. The next step is a 30-minute call with one of our strategists who can walk you through how the model would actually run for your specific vertical and AOV.

Then drop the insider frame:

Okay, can I tell you something? Don't tell anyone I told you this...

Our strategy team isn't going to be pushy or salesy on the call. There's no hard pitch, because the model doesn't need one, it either works for your unit economics or it doesn't. What they WILL do is run your numbers in real time and tell you honestly whether the partnership math works.

Here's the thing: their calendar fills up fast because every brand that finds us wants that conversation. But since I've got you right here, I can get you a head start and put you on the calendar with [Strategist Name], who's been with CPG forever and runs the [vertical] book.

Do you want to do that? Want me to grab 30 minutes for you?

Why this works

  • You're giving them insider access. They feel like CPG is a real operation, not a fly-by-night agency.
  • You're removing the pressure of the pitch. "Not going to be pushy" lowers resistance.
  • You're creating legitimate urgency. Strategist calendars really do fill up fast.
  • You're offering exclusivity. A specific strategist by name, "runs the vertical book."
  • You asked for the yes. "Want me to grab 30 minutes for you?" forces buy-in.

4.6 For purely outbound brands (adaptation)

If you are working a Bison or Instantly reply where the brand hasn't seen any CPG material yet, soften the frame:

What I can do is take all the back-and-forth out of email. Instead of trying to summarise the model in writing, I can get you directly on the calendar with our strategist. They'll walk you through how it works, run your numbers live, and you'll know within 30 minutes whether it makes sense for [Brand Name]. Do you want to do that?

4.7 Booking the call

When they say yes (and roughly 80% will when this is run well):

Awesome, let's pull up calendars. What works better for you, tomorrow morning or tomorrow afternoon?

Critical rules for booking

  • Book within 24 hours whenever possible. Same day or next day. Never book more than 48 hours out unless absolutely necessary (founder is travelling, leadership offsite, etc.). Show rates drop dramatically the further out you book.
  • Give two options (morning or afternoon). Do not give open-ended availability. DTC founders are decision-fatigued.
  • Tell them who they are meeting with and hype the strategist: "You're going to be with Krik. He's been with CPG forever, runs the supplement and beauty book, knows the numbers inside out. You'll be in great hands."
  • Get confirmation: Send the Attio / Calendly calendar invite while on the call. Make sure they receive it. Get a verbal "yes, I got it."

4.8 Ending the call

You're all set. Your call is [day] at [time] with [Strategist Name]. Before then, take 60 seconds and grab your last 90 days of CAC and AOV numbers, even rough ones. That's all you'll need to bring. They'll run the rest from our side.

It was great talking to you. Genuinely excited for this conversation, I think the model is going to map well to what you described. Take care!

Handling Common Responses

Objections

5.1 "Can you just send me a case study first before I get on a call?"

Do not push back. Build the touch point instead.

Totally get that. I'll drop you a one-pager from a brand close to yours, no NDA needed for that one. Hey, is it okay if I reach out to you tomorrow once you've had a look? I'd love to hear what you thought, just for feedback. And if it makes sense, we can grab time with the strategist then.

You just got permission to follow up. You will book them on the back end.

5.2 "Is this a sales call?"

No, no, this isn't a pitch or anything like that. This is literally a conversation with our strategist to look at your specific numbers and tell you whether the affiliate model would actually work for [Brand Name]. No pressure at all. If it's a fit, great. If not, you'll still walk away with a clearer read on your CAC ceiling. Fair enough?

5.3 "I need to talk to my partner / co-founder first."

Totally fair, you should always have the call together. Want me to grab a slot for both of you? That way you're not playing telephone, the strategist can answer both of you in the same conversation. What day next week works for them too?

5.4 "I'm not sure / let me think about it."

Totally fine. I'll drop you a quick one-pager so you can sit with the model on your own time. Hey, if you want to grab time with the strategist after, just text me back and I'll set it up. Sound good?

5.5 "I don't remember filling out a form / signing up for this."

Oh no worries, it happens. We get filled-out forms from someone on the team a lot of the time, ops, growth lead, sometimes even a contractor running paid ads. You're on the list from thecpgaffiliate.com about our performance-based program for DTC brands. If that's not your thing right now, no problem, I'll make sure you're taken care of. But if you ARE thinking about affiliate as a channel, it might be worth a quick look. Want me to send you the one-pager?

5.6 They text "STOP" or "Unsubscribe"

Respect it immediately. Remove them in Attio with the unsubscribed tag. These contacts were never going to buy. Filtering them out actually improves your data quality and protects CPG's sender reputation.

Follow-Up Sequences

Persistence

6.1 Day-after follow-up (for non-bookers)

If a brand operator engaged but did not book during your first call, hit them the next day.

Hey! Was great chatting yesterday but you haven't grabbed your strategy session yet. Ready to book? Don't make me call again 😉 [Setter Name]

If they reply, book them using the same conversational approach, abbreviated for text:

Awesome! Quick question: roughly what's your monthly DTC revenue these days, and are you mostly on Meta or Google for paid? Just so I can pair you with the right strategist on the team.

Get a little info, then: "Perfect, you'll be a great fit for Krik. Let me get you on his calendar."

6.2 The "name + question mark" follow-up

For contacts who never replied to your initial outreach, wait 2 to 3 days, then send just their first name with a question mark.

Sarah?

That's it. It's weird enough that people respond. When they do, re-engage: "Hey! Just checking in, are you still interested in grabbing time with the strategist?"

6.3 The "is this still a priority?" follow-up

Your final attempt before moving to long-term nurture:

Is the affiliate channel still on the roadmap for [Brand Name]?

Or, more direct:

Have you given up on the program idea?

These work. They trigger a response because no one wants to admit they have given up on something they once cared about, especially DTC founders who hate leaving channels on the table.

Show Rate Optimization

Don't Lose The Booking

Once a call is booked, your job is not done. The setter should work hand-in-hand with Attio automation to maximise show rates. DTC founders are interrupt-driven and forget calls more than any other ICP.

7.1 Automated confirmations & reminders

Set up the following in Attio workflows:

  1. Immediately after booking: Confirmation text with date, time, and which strategist they are meeting with. End with: "Reply YES to confirm."
  2. 24 hours before the call: "Friendly reminder, your CPG strategy session is tomorrow at [time]. Bring your last 90 days of CAC and AOV. Does the time still work?"
  3. 4 hours before the call: "Quick reminder, we're on at [time] today. Want me to send the Zoom link again?"
  4. Brands that reply to the 4-hour reminder show up. Brands that ignore all reminders have a significantly lower show rate. This is useful data for your strategist: if a brand confirmed via text, they are warmer and closer to signing.

7.2 The confirmation reply indicator

Track who replies "YES" to confirmations and who does not, in Attio under confirmation_status. Share this with your strategists in the daily handoff. A brand that confirmed is in a different headspace than one that went dark after booking.

DM Setting (LinkedIn & Social)

Inbound DMs

DM setting follows the same philosophical principles as phone setting: genuine curiosity, human connection, qualifying without interrogating. For CPG the primary channel is LinkedIn (where DTC founders actually are), with Instagram as a secondary on consumer-facing brands.

8.1 The first LinkedIn message

  • Trigger: Connection accepted or DM inbound
  • Manual first message: "Thanks for the connect! Saw [specific detail about their brand / role]. What's keeping you busy this quarter?"

Keep the first message specific. No links, no pitches. Just a genuine question grounded in something you actually saw on their profile or in their content. When they reply, you take the conversation forward manually.

8.2 The DM conversation flow

Listen first. Let them tell you what they are focused on, where they are stuck, what they are testing.

Qualify with natural questions:

  • "Are you running affiliate or influencer now, or is that still on the to-do list?"
  • "What's the channel mix looking like right now? Mostly paid or are you getting decent organic too?"
  • "What have you tried before on the partnership side, what didn't click?"

8.3 Routing the prospect

Brand ProfileRoute To
Sub-scale brand, still figuring out core channels, no spend dataOne-pager + quarterly nurture, let them get to scale first
At scale, clear pain point with CAC ceiling, already operating affiliate or wanting toDirect to strategy call, they are ready for a real numbers conversation
Tyre-kicker asking about commission rates with no real intentGive a helpful one-liner, point to thecpgaffiliate.com, don't push

8.4 Booking from DMs

When a brand operator is ready for a call but you are still in DMs, use this transition:

Honestly I could try to type all this out here but it would take 3,500 words and still not really land 😅 Would it be crazy to just hop on a quick call? I can get you 30 minutes with one of our strategists and they can actually walk through whether this model would move the needle for [Brand Name].

8.5 Identity in DMs (running a founder's account)

If you ever operate Leo's or a strategist's LinkedIn, you do not need to announce that you are not them. Most DTC operators understand that anyone with a real book of business has support. If someone directly asks "Is this Leo?", be honest: "Leo is in and out of DMs, but right now you've got me, and I've got you covered."

Never lie. Never pretend to be the principal. But do not volunteer the distinction unless asked.

Compensation Structure

Pay The Setter Properly

A well-compensated setter is a motivated setter. For CPG's offer (performance-based, high LTV partnerships), the recommended structure looks like:

ComponentRecommendation
Base pay$1,500 to $3,000 / month depending on hours and experience
Commission3 to 5% of CPG's first 90 days of revenue from any setter-booked partnership
Trail (optional)1% trailing for 12 months, locks in retention behaviour at the setter level

9.1 Calculating the ROI of a setter

The key metric is DPL (Dollars Per Lead), not cost per lead. The dollar value each booked call represents on a 12-month LTV basis.

Formula: Total partnership revenue (12 mo) ÷ total booked calls = DPL

Example math: Setter books 20 brand calls per month. Strategist closes 25% into partnerships. Average partnership LTV (12 mo) = $30,000. That's $150,000 expected revenue from one month of setter activity. You're paying them $2,500 base + commission. The setter pays for themselves the day they book a partnership that closes.

Even a mediocre setter booking 10 calls a month at a 20% close rate against a $25,000 LTV generates $50,000 in expected revenue against a $3,000 to $4,000 monthly cost. It is very hard to lose money on this hire if they are doing the work.

The Setter and Strategist Relationship

Team Dynamics

This is the piece most operations miss. The setter and the strategists (Krik, Julieta, anyone else who runs calls) must work as a tight unit.

Do

  • Setter posts the day's calendar every morning so every strategist sees what's coming and which vertical
  • Detailed notes go into Attio after every booked call: what the brand sells, AOV, current channel mix, stated pain points
  • Setter and strategist are peers with different specialisations
  • Celebrate wins together, a setter-booked partnership is a team win
  • Over-communicate cancellations, fill slots fast

Don't

  • Treat the setter as subordinate to the strategist
  • Let the strategist go into the call blind, with no Attio notes
  • Hide cancellations or no-shows from the setter
  • Skip the team channel updates because "everyone is busy"
  • Forget that strategists cannot close a brand that never showed up
Bond as a team. Know each other. Trust each other. The strategist cannot sign a brand that never showed up. The setter cannot close a brand they have never met. They need each other.
12

Pre-Call Asset Requirement

Test This

Should you require brand operators to consume a case study, watch a Loom, or fill in a short pre-call intake before their strategy call? This is something to test for CPG specifically.

The testing ladder

  1. Start with no requirement. Anyone can book, no friction.
  2. Track your cost per booking, show rate, and close rate.
  3. Add a requirement to watch a 5-minute Loom from Leo, re-measure.
  4. Add a 3-question pre-call intake form (AOV, current CAC, channel mix), re-measure.
  5. Try requiring both, re-measure.

Find the sweet spot where the friction improves call quality without killing your booking volume. CPG's hypothesis going in: a short intake form will materially lift close rate because the strategist walks in already knowing the unit economics.

What the setter should say regardless

Before the call, grab your last 90 days of CAC and AOV, even rough numbers. The strategist will run the model live, and if you've got those two numbers in front of you it's a way more useful conversation.

Frame the pre-work as something that benefits them, not a gate you are putting up.

Key data point: Brands that bring their numbers to the call close at significantly higher rates. The data does the heavy lifting, it builds trust, establishes that CPG's model is unit-economics-driven, and pre-sells the partnership math. A prepared brand is an easier close.

Using AI for Lead Scoring Analysis

High Leverage

This is a high-leverage move CPG can run quarterly against Attio data.

The process

  1. Export all of your Lead intake data from Attio (form fills, Bison replies, with their ICP labels and metadata)
  2. Export all of your call outcomes (signed, didn't sign, no-show, slow-no, fast-no, etc.)
  3. Feed both data sets to Claude (or another LLM)
  4. Ask: "Which of our intake signals are predictive of whether a brand actually signs?"

What you will likely find

Some signals you think are important (like reported monthly revenue) may have lower predictive power than expected. Others you barely look at (like how many channels they currently run, or whether they have an in-house growth lead) may be strongly predictive. In a benchmark deployment, stated revenue had no statistical correlation with close rate, brands at $50K/mo closed at the same rate as brands at $500K/mo, as long as the other signals lined up.

What to do with this information

  • Remove or replace intake questions that have no predictive value
  • Double down on questions that ARE predictive (and surface those signals to strategists pre-call)
  • Have the LLM suggest new intake questions that might improve data quality
  • Do not let your strategists make assumptions based on intake answers that do not actually predict anything

Run this analysis quarterly as the audience, the verticals you serve, and the offer all evolve.

AI Booking Agent: Implementation

AI Layer

This SOP is written so a human can read it and an AI agent can learn from it. Here is how to translate the principles above into an actual booking agent that runs alongside (or in front of) your setter, using CPG's existing Attio + Bison + cloud function stack.

14.1 What the AI agent should and should not do

AI does well

  • Drafting the first reply within seconds of a Bison reply or form fill
  • Asking the qualifying questions (vertical, AOV, channel mix, current affiliate setup)
  • Suggesting calendar times and sending the Calendly link
  • Sending confirmation messages and reminders
  • Following up at the right cadence (Day 1, Day 2 to 3, Day 5 to 7)
  • Logging every interaction in Attio with structured tags
  • Scoring leads against the CPG ICP before you even respond

AI does badly (keep humans here)

  • The actual phone call. Voice matters, energy matters, humour matters
  • Reading subtle emotional cues like founder burnout, panic, or excitement
  • Spotting a "wrong fit but strategic" brand to escalate manually (PR, partnership, large account referral)
  • Knowing when to break the script to be a real human
  • Telling the difference between a tyre-kicker and a future flagship brand

14.2 Agent system prompt (starter template)

This is a working starter prompt. Adapt the bracketed fields. Feed this SOP into the agent's knowledge base alongside the prompt.

You are the booking agent for CPG Affiliate Partners (thecpgaffiliate.com).

Your job:
1. Reply to inbound brand leads (Bison cold email replies, GAds LP form fills, LinkedIn DMs) within minutes.
2. Qualify each brand against our ICP without sounding like a script.
3. Get qualified brands on the calendar with a CPG strategist inside 48 hours.
4. Hand any edge cases or high-status accounts to a human setter immediately.

Voice:
- Warm, slightly funny, curious, never salesy.
- Short sentences. No corporate jargon. No emojis except 😉 or 😅 when natural.
- Always reference what the brand operator actually wrote. Never generic acknowledgements.
- NEVER use em dashes. Use commas, periods, or hyphens.

ICP (qualify against this):
- DTC consumer brands, $50K to $5M monthly revenue
- AOV $40+
- Already running paid (Meta, Google, or both)
- Verticals we win in: supplements, beauty, food & beverage, pet, home, apparel
- Disqualifiers: services businesses, B2B, marketplace resellers, sub-$10K/mo revenue, AOV under $25, anything FDA-regulated we cannot legally promote

Flow:
1. Acknowledge their message specifically (mention their brand, their vertical, or something they said).
2. Ask 1 to 2 qualifying questions (AOV, channel mix, current affiliate setup).
3. Offer a small free hook (vertical-specific case study one-pager) AND propose a 30 min strategy call.
4. Send Calendly link with two specific time options inside 24 to 48 hours.
5. If they book, send confirmation + 24h reminder + 4h reminder.
6. If they go quiet, follow up Day 1, Day 2 to 3 ("[FirstName]?"), Day 5 to 7 ("Is the channel still on the roadmap?").

Escalate to a human setter when:
- The brand is a known high-LTV account (flagship-tier, $5M+ monthly)
- They ask a technical question outside your knowledge (legal, compliance, FDA, custom commission structure)
- They show emotional weight (frustration with current agency, urgency, anger)
- They explicitly ask to talk to a human

Knowledge base: see attached SOP, Leo's case study library, and the current ICP doc.

14.3 The agent's qualification rubric

Have the agent score each lead before responding. A simple 0 to 5 rubric works.

ScoreBrand ProfileAction
5Perfect ICP, clear intent, founder or growth lead, in target vertical, scaled, paid runningReply within 5 minutes, push for call same day or next day
4Strong ICP, intent unclear but real, decision-maker reachableReply same hour, qualify with one question, push for call inside 48 hours
3Adjacent ICP (smaller, edge vertical), real human, real interestReply same day, send case-study hook, see if conversation develops
2Wrong ICP but strategic (PR opportunity, agency partnership, referral source)Escalate to human (Leo or senior setter), do not auto-reply
1Wrong ICP, low intentSend polite decline or route to quarterly nurture
0Spam, abusive, or unsubscribeTag and remove from list

14.4 Hard rules the agent must never break

  • Never lie. Never claim to be Leo, Krik, or Julieta. Never invent brand testimonials, partnership outcomes, or capabilities.
  • Never quote a commission rate or guarantee. Pricing and structure happen on the call with a human strategist.
  • Never spray identical messages. Every reply must reference something specific the brand operator wrote.
  • Never push past three follow-ups without a response. After Day 7, move to nurture.
  • Never send the full deck, full case study library, or a contract autonomously. Send the one-page anonymised case study only.
  • Never bypass STOP / UNSUBSCRIBE. Tag and remove in Attio on first signal.
  • Never use em dashes in any outbound message. They read as AI tells. Use commas, periods, or hyphens.

14.5 Human-in-the-loop checkpoints

Don't let the agent run fully autonomous until you have at least two weeks of reviewed output. Suggested checkpoints:

  • Week 1: Agent drafts, human approves every send. Build a confidence baseline.
  • Week 2: Agent sends Score 1 to 3 leads autonomously. Score 4 to 5 still draft-then-approve.
  • Week 3+: Agent sends all replies autonomously. Human reviews daily Attio logs.
  • Always: Human handles the actual phone call. The agent never speaks on a call.

14.6 What to feed the agent on day one

  1. This SOP (the whole document)
  2. Your last 50 to 100 winning email replies from Bison and Attio, raw, with outcomes (booked / signed / ghosted)
  3. Your ICP definition in one paragraph (Leo's current version)
  4. Your disqualifiers in one paragraph
  5. Three to five examples of "perfect" replies you would send yourself
  6. Three to five examples of replies that went badly and why
  7. Calendly link, the strategist routing rules (vertical to strategist), and the cases when to surface a specific calendar vs the round-robin
The principle: The agent does not replace the human. It removes the latency between an inbound signal and a thoughtful, qualifying response. The human still owns the phone call, the strategy session, and the partnership relationship. The agent owns the speed.
