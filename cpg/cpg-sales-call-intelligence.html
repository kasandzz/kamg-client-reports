Leaks & Missed Opportunities
31 identified leaks across all calls, ranked by impact

SYSTEMIC LEAKS (appear across multiple calls)

1. No Pre-Call Qualification
Krik consistently doesn't know the prospect's name or company when joining. Full pitches delivered to prospects who don't qualify (Avira Diamonds: no US shipping; Raw Vision: $10 CPA). A basic intake form asking "current Meta CPA" and "do you ship to US?" would eliminate wasted call slots.
Affected calls: Codex Labs (1st), Avira Diamonds, Raw Vision, Ignite Attachments, CSN Mint | Impact: HIGH
2. "Ask the CEO" Bottleneck
Julieta and Erica defer flat fees, influencer deals, escrow alternatives, referral models, and any non-standard request to Krik. This creates a decision bottleneck and signals to prospects that they're not talking to someone with authority. CPG needs pre-approved decision trees for the top 5 non-standard requests.
Affected calls: Vanity Couture, Partnermatic, Cult Content, Olifant Digital, Franceska | Impact: HIGH
3. No Hard Next-Step Dates
Almost every call ends with "I'll send you info" or "I'll follow up" -- no specific date, no calendar hold, no commitment. This is pipeline leakage 101. Every call should end with "I'll send the proposal by [date] and let's reconnect [day] at [time]."
Affected calls: 80%+ of all calls | Impact: HIGH
4. Margin Disclosure
Both Krik and Julieta disclosed CPG's margin structure unprompted: "We go to market at $15 CPA and make the $5 extra" (Raw Vision), "On $20, I'm making $3-7" (Sleep AI). This is proprietary and unnecessary -- the prospect only needs to know their CPA.
Affected calls: Raw Vision, Sleep AI, JALG | Impact: MEDIUM
5. Casino Offers in Standard Pitch
Erica mentions "we also have casino offers" in every network pitch regardless of audience -- health apps, neurodivergent communities, tech publishers. This is contextually inappropriate and signals lack of audience awareness.
Affected calls: Qrkiez, Techpoint/TNW, multiple publisher calls | Impact: MEDIUM
6. Restrictions Before Discovery
The scripted pitch states "we do not accept incentivized traffic" as a hard rule before understanding the prospect's model. This nearly killed the AMP Consulting deal (their entire model is soft-incent). Qualify first, then state relevant restrictions.
Affected calls: AMP Consulting, Affluxo | Impact: MEDIUM
7. No Live Proof During Calls
Zero calls included a live case study, Everflow demo, or proof point. Everything is pushed post-call ("I'll send you a deck"). Showing a 30-second Everflow dashboard screenshot or one case study stat during the call would dramatically increase conversion.
Affected calls: ALL | Impact: HIGH

The CPG Playbook: What's Actually Working
CPG has built an effective but inconsistent sales motion. The core differentiators are genuine and powerful:
  1. Founder-operator credibility -- Krik's story is authentic and disarms skepticism faster than any deck
  2. Anti-cheat framework -- The 3-condition model + "agencies drive repeat customers" reveal is a genuine insight most prospects have never heard

The leaks are process, not product. Fix the pre-call qualification, give reps decision authority on the top 5 non-standard scenarios, add one live proof point per call, and close with specific dates. The pitch itself is strong.
