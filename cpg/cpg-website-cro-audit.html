Section 01

Executive
Summary

McKinsey Pyramid structure. Situation -- Complication -- Resolution.

Situation

CPG Affiliate Partners operates a performance-based affiliate marketing agency targeting DTC e-commerce brands ($2--20M revenue). The website serves as the primary lead generation channel for a high-touch, consultation-based sales model. The market is competitive: Acceleration Partners, Advertise Purple, Hamster Garage, PartnerCentric, Gen3, and DMi Partners all compete for the same sophisticated DTC buyer.

Complication

The website has four critical structural failures that collectively suppress conversion: (1) no homepage lead capture mechanism, (2) a rotating hero with no clear value proposition, (3) a "Numbers That Speak For Themselves" section with no visible numbers, and (4) zero case studies despite 11 CEO testimonials. The conversion path requires 3--4 clicks minimum to reach a 6-field form with a "Submit" button. Sophisticated B2B buyers evaluating multiple agencies cannot self-qualify or understand CPG's differentiation.

Resolution

15 specific recommendations organized by severity follow. Estimated conversion lift: 80--150% from implementing critical fixes alone. The underlying credibility assets exist -- Krik's 2x exit story, 11 CEO testimonials with specific results, a performance-only model. The gap is deployment, not substance.

Section 08

Behavioral Psychology
Playbook

Six interventions grounded in Rory Sutherland's psycho-logic and applied persuasion research.

1
APP-012 / RS-007
Costly Signaling
CPG uses cheap signals ("great results", "trusted by brands") instead of costly ones. Cheap signals are easy to fake -- therefore they're discounted. Costly signals require real investment or genuine capability, making them credible by definition.
Fix: "508 verified Shopify orders tracked this month" is a costly signal. Show live dashboard numbers publicly. Name the technology. Let specificity do the trust work.
2
APP-010
Reframing
CPG frames itself as "an affiliate agency" -- a commodity category. The frame determines the comparison set. In the "affiliate agency" frame, CPG competes on price and reviews against agencies with more of both.
Fix: Reframe as "the only affiliate partner built by founders who've exited two CPG brands." New category. CPG is the only entrant.
3
APP-003 / RS-015
Default Effect
The next step is currently a choice: "Contact us IF you want." Most visitors won't exercise that choice. Defaults are powerful because they require no decision.
Fix: Make action the default. "Your free affiliate audit starts here" -- with a form already visible -- frames inaction as opting out, not action as opting in.
4
APP-005
Strategic Friction
Zero qualification signals anyone can contact CPG. When anything is available to everyone, it feels less valuable. The Doorman Effect: exclusivity at entry increases desire for what's inside.
Fix: Add a qualifying question. "We only work with brands doing $2M+" makes qualifying brands feel selected, not sold to. Rejection risk creates perceived value.
5
APP-011
Specificity as Credibility
Every claim on the site is generic. "Results-driven" means nothing. Specific numbers are harder to fabricate -- therefore, brains treat them as inherently more credible. Vague claims trigger vague trust.
Fix: "43% average CPA reduction across 25 DTC brands in 2025" is a completely different trust signal than "great results." Audit every claim and attach a number.
6
CF-018
In-Group Identity
"Built for brands, by brand owners" triggers tribal identity -- the most powerful persuasion force in human psychology. People trust people demonstrably like them. "As brand owners ourselves" is doing almost no work on this site.
Fix: Current copy says "brands and publishers" -- split identity that resonates with neither group. Identity messaging (+31%) belongs in the H1 and every ad.
