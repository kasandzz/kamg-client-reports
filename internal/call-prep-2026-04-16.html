Section 02

Why 0.42% Conversion Rate (and Why It's Fixable)

The ads are working. The landing experience is not. Here's the honest breakdown.

Factor Verdict Explanation
Click-Through Rate (5%) Strong Industry average is 1-2%. We're at 5%. People ARE clicking the ads. The ad copy and keyword targeting are working.
Cost Per Click Good ~$4.68/click is reasonable for B2B manufacturing. Not the problem.
Landing Page Experience Root Cause Ads send traffic to the current homepage which was not built for ad conversion. No keyword-matched headlines, weak form positioning, no service-specific pages. This is where 99.6% of visitors drop off.
Conversion Tracking (historical) Was Inflated Previous tracking counted page views and FAQ clicks as "conversions." We cleaned this -- the 0.42% is now the REAL number. Before it looked better but was dishonest.
Quality Score Low Landing page relevance is the biggest Quality Score factor. When homepage changes, QS will improve, which means lower CPCs and better ad positions -- same budget, more results.
Ad-to-Page Match Mismatched Someone searches "die casting Mexico," clicks the ad, lands on a generic homepage. They should land on a die casting-specific page with specs, certs, and a quote form. Service-specific LPs are in progress.
The fix: Launch the new homepage + service-specific landing pages. Based on industry benchmarks and the COD case study (where LP changes lifted conversion 50% in 3 days), we expect conversion rate to improve 2-5x once the new pages are live. That would take $1,115/conversion down to $220-$550 range.
