01 / Executive Summary

The Opportunity Gap

Situation

The Iron Initiative has the architecture of a premium executive health coaching program -- a compelling webinar-led offer, specific case studies with measurable biomarker outcomes, and a credentialed coach with a verifiable sports performance background. The $10K-20K+ price point is defensible in a market where competitors charge $5K-$30K routinely. The offer is not the problem.

Complication

The funnel is structured as if trust has already been established before the visitor arrives. There is zero social proof before any ask. The registration page leads with a 5-minute video on mobile -- a format that executive audiences abandon immediately. Testimonials appear only on the Thank You page, after commitment. The booking page is a bare calendar widget with no preparation, no objection handling, and no show-rate optimization. The result is a leaky funnel at every stage.

Resolution

The rebuild inverts the trust architecture. Proof appears before the ask. The booking page transforms into a pre-call preparation experience that increases show rates. Mobile-first design eliminates the video friction problem. A genuine scarcity mechanism tied to cohort enrollment replaces the current vacuum. With these changes, a 2-4x conversion lift is a conservative projection based on documented evidence from comparable high-ticket funnels.

04 / Critical Findings

Five Failures, Five Fixes

These are not suggestions. Each finding represents a measurable conversion leak with documented evidence from comparable funnels.

01
Critical

Zero Social Proof Before Any Ask

The registration page and training pages contain no testimonials, credentials, authority signals, or social validation of any kind. In a $10K-20K+ market, a visitor arrives as a skeptic. Proof is the mechanism that converts skeptics into applicants. Without it, the page is asking for trust it has not earned. The 76ers credential, biomarker case studies, and named client outcomes all exist -- none appear where they would convert.

Evidence
Social proof testing (CXL Case #198): +64.5% CVR when proof added above the fold. Named testimonials outperform anonymous by 34% in high-ticket contexts.
Recommended Fix
Add credential badge (76ers), 2 named case studies with biomarker numbers, and a testimonial pull-quote directly below the headline on registration page.
02
Critical

5-Minute Video Gate on Mobile = Mass Exit

90% of incoming traffic is mobile. The current funnel requires visitors to watch a 5-minute video before they can proceed. On mobile, this is a conversion wall. Executive audiences will not wait. Autoplay is muted by default on mobile browsers, so the video often plays silently -- removing its persuasion function entirely while still demanding attention. This single friction point is likely responsible for the majority of funnel abandonment.

Evidence
Video on LP (Crazy Egg Case #258): +64% CVR -- but only when video is optional, not a gate. Muted autoplay on mobile eliminates persuasion value while keeping friction intact.
Recommended Fix
Integrate video into VSL/application landing page with click-to-play on mobile. Move persuasion copy alongside video so mobile users who skip video still convert. Never gate progress behind video completion.
03
Critical

Testimonials Appear After Commitment (Backwards Architecture)

Social proof currently appears on the Thank You page -- after the visitor has already registered. This is precisely backwards. Proof is a pre-commitment tool. Its job is to reduce perceived risk and increase confidence before the decision point. Showing testimonials post-registration is a wasted asset. It confirms a decision already made. The Thank You page should be used for show-rate optimization, not proof delivery.

Evidence
Proof-first architecture is standard in all high-converting high-ticket funnels. Positioning proof before the CTA is a foundational principle, not an optimization.
Recommended Fix
Move all testimonials and case studies to the registration page, training page form section, and booking page. Repurpose Thank You page for show-rate content: what to prepare, what to expect, set expectations for the call.
04
High

Bare Calendar Widget = No-Show Factory

The booking page presents a calendar widget with no surrounding context, no show-rate optimization, no pre-call preparation content, and no objection handling. A bare booking page treats the scheduled call as the destination. It is not -- the attended call is the destination. Executives who book do not always show. Pre-call preparation content, expectation-setting, and light commitment devices are the mechanism that separates a filled calendar from a productive one.

Evidence
Pre-call homework and preparation content (HT-09): 94% show rate + 2x close rate on attended calls. Calendar-only pages typically see 40-60% no-show rates on cold traffic.
Recommended Fix
Redesign booking page as a 2-column layout: calendar widget on one side, pre-call prep content on the other. Include what to prepare, what the call covers, and a testimonial from a past call participant.
05
High

No Urgency Mechanism Anywhere in the Funnel

Executive buyers are conditioned to delay. Without a genuine urgency mechanism, "I'll think about it" is the default outcome. The current funnel has no scarcity signals, no cohort enrollment windows, no capacity limitations, and no deadline-based motivation. This is particularly damaging in a coaching context where the offer itself is naturally scarce -- one coach, limited client capacity -- but that natural scarcity is never communicated. Manufactured urgency backfires; genuine scarcity converted by a factor of 3x.

Evidence
Genuine scarcity (CF-016): +332% CR vs manufactured urgency which showed +17% then -12% after pattern recognition. Cohort-based enrollment is the most defensible genuine scarcity mechanism for coaching.
Recommended Fix
Introduce cohort-based enrollment with a genuine client capacity number (e.g., "8 spots per cohort"). Display remaining spots on registration and application pages. Tie to real intake windows.

07 / Behavioral Playbook

The Psychology Layer

Rory Sutherland interventions applied to specific conversion moments. These are the behavioral mechanisms underpinning the rebuilt pages.

APP-005
The Application IS the Sale
The application form creates strategic friction that self-selects serious buyers and generates the IKEA effect -- investment in the process increases perceived value of the outcome. A multi-step application is not a barrier; it is a qualifier that makes acceptance feel earned.
Training / Application Page
APP-011
Specificity Signals Credibility
Exact biomarker numbers (T levels in ng/dL, specific peptide protocols, named measurements) do more credibility work than general claims. "650 ng/dL" is more persuasive than "testosterone increased dramatically." Executives process specificity as a proxy for competence.
All Pages
APP-010
Reframe the Investment
$10-20K sounds large until it is reframed as the cheapest form of insurance on the most important asset the prospect owns: their body. Executive audiences respond to ROI language. The cost of not investing (diminished performance, health decline, lost productivity) must be made visceral before the price is introduced.
Application Form -- Investment Question
CF-016
Genuine Scarcity Outperforms Manufactured
The Iron Initiative has natural, genuine scarcity: one coach, limited client slots per cohort. This reality should be made explicit and quantified. Manufactured countdown timers backfire when audiences recognize them. Genuine capacity constraints -- stated plainly -- converted at 332% above baseline in controlled testing.
Registration + Application Page

The Two Reasons Framework

APP-013: Every executive prospect has a stated reason for wanting this program and a real reason. Both must be addressed. The stated reason gets the meeting; the real reason closes it.

The Good Reason (Stated)
Performance Optimization
"I want to have more energy for my team, perform at a higher level, and set a physical standard as a leader." This is the reason they will write in the application. It is real, but it is not the driver.
The Real Reason (Unstated)
Fear of Decline
"I am 47. I can feel the gap widening between where I am and where I need to be. I am afraid that if I do not do something now, I will not be able to recover." Copy must acknowledge this fear without triggering shame. The program is the antidote to decline, not just a performance upgrade.
