Positioning — Boris's exact framing

From Nov 2025 campaign feedback

"They do not need us or anyone to make them CGI. Their real need is: save money, save time, keep quality, keep creative control, help with smaller tasks to finish marketing campaigns in time, and unload their team."

Lead with their pain, not LTHS capability. The agency or brand already knows what CGI / AI video is. The reply trigger is whether you've named the actual headache: timeline pressure, internal team overload, freelancer quality variance, agency pricing.

Voice rules

Do

  • Short. 50–75 words for email #1.
  • Lowercase, internal-feeling subject lines (2–4 words). Examples: quick question, creative bandwidth, q3 video.
  • One observation about them, one credibility line, one soft ask.
  • Contractions. "We've," "you're," "I'd."
  • Name AI explicitly when relevant — it's closing the deals right now.
  • Acknowledge they likely have an internal team or agency. Don't pretend they're starting from zero.

Don't

  • Em dashes. Anywhere. They're an AI tell and Kas's house ban applies here too.
  • "I hope this email finds you well," "I came across your profile," "leverage," "synergy," "best-in-class."
  • Open with "My name is X and I work at Y."
  • Reference brands LTHS hasn't actually worked with. Brand list above is the ceiling.
  • Pitch every service in one email. One angle per send.

Subject lines that have worked

  • quick question
  • video bandwidth
  • creative pipeline
  • ai video idea
  • {first name}, quick one — only if data is verified

Three angles to rotate across email #1 sends

  1. Capacity angle: "Your team is already running. We add a lane for the heavy creative weeks without a long onboarding."
  2. Speed angle: "AI-assisted video means you ship the testing volume Meta needs, not the one hero we usually see from agencies."
  3. Cost angle: "Same studio quality without the studio rate card. Brands like {one of the four} run their cinematic work this way."
03

Embed proof in the inbox · Images and signature logos

New tactic

The strongest LTHS asset is the work itself. A frame from the M&Ms or Sims spot lands harder than three sentences describing it. Goal: show prospects what they'll get without asking them to click anything.

When to insert images

TouchImage useWhy
Email #1 (cold)No body image. Logo strip in signature only.Inline images in first sends hurt deliverability and trip "marketing email" filters. Signature strip is small, branded, low-risk.
Email #2 (follow-up, +3 days)One inline image: video still or 3-frame mini-loop. ~600px wide.By touch 2 the sender domain is no longer cold to that mailbox. Inline media has been received before. Now the visual proof works.
Email #3 (case-study angle)Single still from one named brand spot. Caption: brand name + 1-line outcome.Specificity beats variety. One image, one credit, one number.
Email #4 (breakup)No image. Plain text.Breakup is a tone shift. Strip the visuals to signal honesty.

Image specs

  • Format: JPG or PNG. No animated GIF until we test deliverability impact. Animated GIF can also fail to render in Outlook desktop.
  • Width: 600px max display, 1200px source for retina. Keep aspect 16:9 or square.
  • File size: under 100 KB per image. Compress with Squoosh or ImageOptim before upload.
  • Hosting: serve from a domain LTHS controls (CDN or lths.studio subdomain), not third-party image hosts. Reduces "spammy referrer" flags.
  • Alt text: always set. alt="LTHS x EA — The Sims cinematic frame". Accessibility plus a fallback when images are blocked.
  • One image per email. Stacked images = newsletter pattern = deliverability hit.

Personalized image variant (test, not default)

Lemlist / Smartlead have a feature where the prospect's logo or name is overlaid onto a brand image. Useful for marketing-agency outreach because their team already sells visuals.

  • Test cell: 200 sends, marketing-agency segment only. Half with personalized image, half with static still.
  • Read after: 7 days. Compare reply rate AND deliverability (inbox vs spam).
  • Kill criteria: if personalized variant drops inbox rate below 90% — turn off, the static-image path stays.

Deliverability guardrails

  • Keep the text-to-image ratio above 80% text in any email that has an image.
  • One link per email. Boris's website only. No tracking-pixel-style image hosts.
  • Warm any new domain or mailbox 14+ days before it sends an image-bearing email.
  • Run a Mail-Tester score before launching the image variant. Target 9.5+/10.
  • Watch the Apr 20 campaign's spam-folder rate in week 1 of the image rollout. Pause and revert if open rate drops more than 15% off baseline.

Reply handling · Pre-forward filter and CC discipline

Protect Boris's inbox

Boris's pushback in 2025 was that the "interested prospect" bot called too many soft "no" replies positive, and that low-LTV single-product replies clogged his decisioning time. The fix is a pre-forward filter on Vovik's side.

Pre-forward decision tree

Reply patternForward?Where
Multi-SKU brand · explicit interest · asks for portfolio / pricing / callForward immediatelyBoris + CC Vladislav
Marketing / performance agency · interested or curiousForward immediatelyBoris + CC Vladislav
FB-advertiser brand · warm but not now · "keep on file"Forward, label "nurture"Boris + Vladislav, plus Vovik nurture queue
Single-product e-commerce · interestedHoldVovik's hold folder. Quarterly digest to Boris, not 1:1 forwards.
Soft "no" / "not now" with no follow-up triggerHoldHold for 90 days. Re-engage on a different angle.
Hard "no" / "remove me"SuppressSuppression list. Never re-import.
Off-fit (called out targeting accuracy, e.g. wrong industry)Suppress + auditAdd to suppression. Flag the source list to Vovik so it stops generating mismatches.

Forwarding format (every fit reply)

  • Subject prefix Boris asked for: use the prefix Boris pinned in the Slack channel so his Gmail filters route correctly. (Vovik confirmed Apr 27 that Vladislav is now CC'd on all forwards — keep that.)
  • Two-line summary above the original thread: "Multi-SKU DTC, FB advertiser, asks for pricing. Reply suggested: confirm portfolio link + propose 15 min."
  • Tag the campaign source: [AI video, Retail EU] or [Marketing Agencies]. Helps Boris see which campaign is producing what.

Authenticity guardrail

If a prospect asks "have you worked with X?" — never confirm a brand that's not on the locked list. Default reply: "Our public case studies are with Amazon MGM Studio, M&Ms, GAP, and EA. Other work is under NDA — happy to walk you through on a call."

Reporting · Funnel metrics Boris asked for

Retention lever

Boris's literal ask: "how many emails sent vs landed in my mailbox vs converted." His unsaid version: "I want to know this is working before I keep paying." Closing that visibility gap is the single biggest retention move on this account right now.

Metrics for the dashboard v1 (Leo is building)

MetricDefinitionSource
SentEmails dispatched per campaign per dayVovik's sender platform export (Smartlead / Instantly / Woodpecker)
Delivered (inbox-landed)Sent minus bounces minus spam-folder estimateSender platform + sample inbox checks
Reply rateTotal replies / delivered. Split by sentiment.Vovik labels every reply as positive / warm / negative / off-fit
Forwarded to BorisReplies that passed the pre-forward filter (Section 4)Vovik's forward log
BookedCalls scheduled with BorisBoris's calendar / Vladislav
ClosedContracts signed, attributed back to source campaignBoris confirms in Slack

Cadence Vovik should hit

  • Daily: sender-platform export pushed to a shared sheet (or Vovik's existing dashboard) at end of day.
  • Weekly: short Slack summary per active campaign — sends, delivered, replies (split), forwarded, off-fit count, top 3 prospects.
  • Per launch: day-1 and day-3 deliverability check on any new campaign or new mailbox.

What this unblocks

Once these numbers are visible to Boris, two things stop being friction: (1) the implicit anxiety about whether the spend is working, and (2) the ICP debate becomes data-led instead of opinion-led. Whichever vertical drives the highest forwarded-to-booked ratio wins the next budget cycle.
