What we are building

A custom Astro build, Claude Code-driven — not a WordPress refresh. The decision is to move off WordPress entirely so SEO architecture, programmatic pages, and per-page schema are owned in code, not plugins.

The new site follows a Fictiv / Xometry hub-and-spoke pattern at Redstone scale. Three hubs: Capabilities, Industries, Resources. Homepage = conversion hub. Service / geo pages absorb ad traffic. Resources hub is the long-term moat. Existing 85 posts get re-homed into Resources, not killed.

Why this plan, why now

  • SEO is recoverable but capped at ~50 pages. The 2,000 organic sessions / mo cluster on technical material guides (st37, ss400, adc12) that Google's AI Overview is now eating. Need commercial-intent depth.
  • Ads are bleeding. $819 / lead because LPs aren't shipped at scale. Service LPs unlock ad performance.
  • Eric's 30-day performance clock started Apr 17. Site rebuild has to support, not slow, that test.
  • The Mexico / tariff angle is uncontested. Neither Fictiv nor Xometry owns it. This is Redstone's blue ocean.

04  The architecture decision

Hub-and-spoke, three hubs, one form

Hub 1 — Capabilities

The transactional hub. Each top-3 service (die casting, CNC, sheet metal) gets a deep capability page. Each capability page links to a Daniel-ad-group LP variant for paid traffic, plus geo variants, plus 3–5 spoke articles for organic.

7 service hubs » 30+ spokes

Hub 2 — Industries

The vertical-intent hub. Four pages: Automotive (IATF16949), Aerospace (AS9100 if applicable), Defense (US facility), Medical. Each maps to actual Redstone certifications and links into the matching capabilities.

4 industry hubs » 8–12 spokes

Hub 3 — Resources

The authority hub. Pillar guides (die casting, CNC, sheet metal), the Mexico nearshoring guide, the existing material spec pages (kept), case studies, and a tariff thought-leadership cluster. This is the long-term moat.

5 pillars » 25+ material/spec/comparison spokes

One form, every page

Same multi-step quote form (Project / Specs / Contact) on every capability and geo LP. File upload required (CAD / STEP / IGES). Step 1 quietly filters: Estimated Annual Volume screens out sub-$50K without saying "minimum order $50K." Tariff-inclusive estimate framing throughout.

One trust bar, above the fold, every page

ISO 9001 Certified IATF 16949 Certified Helping Companies Since 2018

No "factories." No "global network." No "marketplace" or "not a marketplace." Always "ISO 9001" in copy, never generic "ISO-certified."

The conversion path, simplified

Google Ad ↓ [Service or Geo LP] — trust bar ↑ · match-to-keyword H1 · multi-step form · Mexico facility framing ↓ Quote Request (file upload) ↓ HubSpot deal — "qualified" stage gates whether it counts as a lead ↓ Eric's sales process Organic ↓ [Resource pillar] → internal link to [Capability hub] → same form [Material spec] → internal link to relevant [Capability hub] → same form

09  SEO + conversion hybrid pattern

What we lift from each competitor

From Omnius (CRO architecture)

  • Hub-and-spoke topology. Every page belongs to a hub. Footer is a 50-link silo.
  • Multi-CTA entry. Hero, mid-page, sticky rail, footer. No single chokepoint.
  • Specificity over claims. "8–12 wk lead time" beats "fast." "±0.001"" beats "tight tolerances."
  • Layered logo bar. Add industry context (auto, aerospace, OEM) under each logo.
  • "Get started" form pattern. Multi-step, tiny first ask, escalates.
  • Schema.org aggregate rating (when we have testimonial volume).

From Fictiv (vertical depth)

  • Industry vertical pages as a real hub, not afterthought tiles.
  • Material×process programmatic SEO. "CNC machining aluminum," "Die casting zinc," etc.
  • Named case studies with metrics in the title (Honeywell 7× faster, Halliburton $1.2M).
  • Resource hub as a top-level nav item, not a blog backwater.
  • Real-people authoring (E-E-A-T) — Eric and ops leads quoted on technical pages.
  • "We are a manufacturer, not a platform" framing — Redstone's structural advantage over Fictiv itself.

From Xometry (selective programmatic)

  • Encyclopedic pillar pages on the top-3 services. 3K–5K words.
  • Comparison content. "Die casting vs sand casting vs investment casting."
  • Tolerance / standards reference pages — engineers bookmark and link.
  • FAQ schema everywhere — goal is AI-Overview citation, not informational click capture (which AI eats).
  • Skip: instant quote engine, 5K–10K page volume, design-guide eBook gating.

What we explicitly do NOT copy

  • Omnius's green / blue accent. Redstone's house style is red on grayscale.
  • Omnius's SaaS / fintech vertical lens. Redstone is industrial — serious, not slick.
  • Fictiv's "platform" narrative. Redstone is a manufacturer with owned facilities — lead with that.
  • Xometry's informational query strategy. AI Overviews now eat that traffic. Commercial-intent only.
  • Xometry's page volume. 5K pages is a marketplace strategy, not a manufacturer one.
  • Resource gating with email walls — Eric's ICP doesn't want to opt-in for a guide. Free, indexable, on-page.

Redstone's 3 unfair advantages (per Fictiv teardown)

  • Real, named facilities. Photos, locations, named ops leads — Fictiv structurally cannot do this.
  • Tariff / nearshoring expertise. Eric's daily sales pitch. Zero competitor coverage.
  • Casting depth. Fictiv is thin on casting; Xometry is broad-but-shallow. Redstone owns this niche.

Every page should reinforce at least one of these three. If it doesn't, cut it.
