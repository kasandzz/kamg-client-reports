How to use this guide

This is the single source of truth for the Redstone website rebuild. Every team member, designer, frontend dev, copywriter, UX, QA, reads this before touching a page. It tells you what we are building, why, and what each page must contain. The PRD is the engineering spec. This guide is the meaning behind it.

If you are designing

Read sections 2 to 8 then the page card you are designing. Honor Eric's rules at all times.

If you are wireframing

Find the page in section 9, 10, or 11. The "required sections" line tells you the blocks to lay out.

If you are coding

Read the PRD for the technical contract, then the page card here for content + intent.

If you are writing copy

Section 6 is your bible. Eric's banned words list is non-negotiable. Match the H1 to the primary keyword.

If you are doing UX

Section 12 is the conversion engine, the quote form. Sections 7 + 8 are the templates and components you assemble from.

If you are QA

Section 14 is performance gates, section 5 is Eric's rules. A page is not done until both pass.

The mindset. Redstone's homepage rebuild and 71 supporting pages exist for one reason. Eric loses leads because the existing site does not show up for the searches that matter. Jack found 90 keywords representing 47,990 monthly searches that we are not on. This rebuild is the SEO machine that captures them, plus the conversion surface that turns the click into a quote. Every page choice should serve those two outcomes.
Section 2

The vision in one minute

Redstone Manufacturing is a U.S. company that makes metal parts at certified foundries in Mexico, India, China, Brazil, and Taiwan. Engineering, project management, and account ownership sit in the U.S. Production happens overseas. The buyer gets American-quality accountability with overseas-pricing economics. That is the entire story.

What we are building

  • 71 pages across services, geography, and resources, on the same domain, in 90 days.
  • Phase 0 (Days 1 to 30): Homepage rebuild + every country page (Mexico, India, China, Brazil) + their service spokes. These are the lowest-difficulty keywords and rank fastest.
  • Phase 1 (Days 31 to 60): Service hubs (die casting, CNC, sheet metal, etc.) + material spokes + comparison resources + utility pages (about, contact, quote, case study).
  • Phase 2 (Days 61 to 90): HVAC sheet metal, prototype sheet metal, and conditional industry pages (automotive, aerospace) only if the certifications exist.

What success looks like

  • 71 pages indexed by Day 90
  • Hosting fully off hosting.com before May 13
  • Zero traffic regression on existing material guides (st37, ss400, adc12, etc., ~2K sessions/mo)
  • Quote form is the primary conversion surface, multi-step with CAD upload, posts to HubSpot
  • Lighthouse 90+ everywhere, zero Pa11y blockers
  • FAQ schema on every service hub and comparison page so we earn AI Overview citations instead of being cannibalized by them

What we are NOT doing

  • No visual brand redesign. Existing palette, ZT Nature font, no new logo.
  • No CMS. Astro content collections + markdown frontmatter is the system.
  • No multi-language at launch.
  • No blog. Resource pages are decision tools, not a publishing engine.
  • No new sections invented from scratch. Use the kamg-astro-starter component library first, always.

Internal linking pattern (the Omnius rule)

  1. Service hub points down to its material spokes. Each spoke links back up to its hub.
  2. Service hub points sideways to geo variants of that service. Each geo page links back to the service hub.
  3. Service hub points to 1-2 comparison resources. Each resource page CTAs to the service it solves for.
  4. Footer carries the full silo, all 7 service hubs + 4 geo hubs + resources hub. Keeps the nav bar clean while preserving topical density.
  5. Anchor text mix, ~5 to 10% exact match, 10 to 20% partial, 70 to 85% natural/contextual, 1 to 2% branded.
Section 5

Eric's hard rules, read these first

These are non-negotiable. If a page violates any of these, it does not ship. Eric will catch it. Most of these come from the April 9 marketing review, the onboarding call, and Daniel Hall's PPC brief.

Positioning rules

RuleWhy it exists
Redstone is a U.S. company that manufactures overseas. Not a sourcing agent, not a marketplace, not a broker. Always frame as "U.S. company with overseas production." Eric (Apr 9): "I don't want to seem like we are a sourcing company connecting them to factories." Xometry, Protolabs, Fictiv are brokers. Redstone is a manufacturer.
The hook is "American quality with overseas pricing." Approved variant: "The trustworthiness of an American company with the pricing from overseas." Approved by Eric and Rachel on the March 31 call. This is the entire elevator pitch in one sentence.
Above the fold on every service + geo page must contain ISO 9001 + IATF 16949 + "Helping companies since 2018." Daniel Hall mandate, repeated by Eric. Without these proofs the page reads as a comparison site (which Eric explicitly rejects).
U.S. + Canada audience only. No EU, no Asia, no LATAM as primary audience. Eric in onboarding. Past EU experiments did not convert.
$50K minimum order value, volume only. No prototypes, no one-offs, no hobbyists. Eric: "we don't take orders that are smaller than $50,000." Quote form must filter for this implicitly.
Use real cities, not regions. "Monterrey" beats "Mexico." "Shanghai" beats "China." "Mumbai" beats "India." Eric (Apr 9): city specificity = operations proof. Cam (previous SEO) was deliberate about local addresses for local-business signal.
One U.S. point of contact end-to-end. No account-manager handoffs, no time-zone gaps. Eric's positioning. Resolves the "where is this made / who is accountable" anxiety.

Copy and CTA rules

RuleWhy
No "global" anywhere. Not "global manufacturing," not "global network," not "global partner." Eric (Apr 9): "Any place that says, like, global network, we need to change how we're framing it."
No "factories." Use "supplier," "manufacturer," "foundry," "facility." Eric: implies sourcing-agent positioning, which he rejects.
No "precision engineering." Eric: "we don't do engineering." Redstone manufactures from supplied designs, does not design.
No tariff-led messaging in hero. Tariff context is OK as a comparison-table block deeper in the page (Mexico vs China). Do not lead the hero with tariffs. Eric (Apr 9): asked about leading with tariff angle, replied "No" (one word, strongest signal in the call).
No "build it" as a CTA. Use "Request Quote," "Start My Project," "Move Into Production." Eric: "People don't build with our parts. It's like production, something related to like starting production."
No "Talk to a mechanical engineer" as a deep-page CTA. Eric: deep-page CTAs assume commitment, use production language.
Progressive CTA ladder. Hero says "Request Quote." Mid-page says "Start My Project." Final/sticky says "Move Into Production." Eric agreed on Apr 9. Each step assumes more buyer commitment.
No superlatives. Banned: "world-class," "best-in-class," "premier," "industry-leading," "premium." Engineer voice. Numbers beat adjectives. Eric is allergic to marketing fluff.
No em dashes. Anywhere. House style is comma or period. Em dashes read as AI-tells in client-facing copy. Permanent KAMG rule.
No "made in USA," "US-based die casting," "American manufacturing," "domestic foundry." Redstone manufactures offshore. Cannot fulfill domestic-only intent profitably.
No bare "CNC machining" as an ad target or H1. Use qualified variants: "CNC manufacturing," "CNC machining company," "custom CNC machining." Eric: bare term has too-vague intent.
No "low volume CNC" or "prototype CNC" pages. Filters out Redstone's ICP. Eric specifically excluded.
No "nearshore manufacturing" pages. Total search volume ~40/mo, wastes effort. Use "Mexico manufacturing" or "Monterrey production" instead.
No firearms imagery. Even though firearms is a real Redstone industry, Google Ads policy excludes it from any ad creative or LP image. Compliance.
No "Seattle." Redstone is Ohio-HQ'd. Use "U.S. HQ" or "U.S. headquarters" if HQ city is unclear. Factual. Past copy mistakenly used Seattle.

RuleWhy

Section 6

Brand voice in 60 seconds

Tone descriptors

  1. Engineer-to-engineer. Copy is read by engineers. No consumer/marketing register.
  2. Spec-first. Numbers before adjectives. Tolerance, volume, lead time, cert ID, always pick a number over a claim.
  3. Cautiously under-promised. Say 20%, not 30%. Say "consistently short," not "industry-leading."
  4. Direct, plainspoken. Short sentences. One idea per line. No jargon stacking.
  5. Proof-led. Lead with certifications, tenure, and named clients. Proof before claim, not after.

Approved copy examples

  • "The trustworthiness of an American company with the pricing from overseas." (the hook)
  • "ISO-certified facilities. Done." (Eric verbatim, two words plus a period)
  • "Manufacturing facilities across the U.S., Mexico, and Asia. One U.S. point of contact." (Eric verbatim)
  • "Same-day technical feasibility review." (process promise, specific not vague)
  • "High-volume die casting with ISO-certified tolerance control. Aluminum, zinc, magnesium." (capability frame, all spec)

The 6 page templates

Six layouts cover the entire 71-page site. Each template is one Astro layout. Variation comes from frontmatter, not from new files. If you find yourself building a 7th template, stop and ask why.

Geography hub 4 The country page (e.g., /mexico/). Hero, facility map, country-specific messaging (USMCA for Mexico, QC oversight for India/China, completeness for Brazil), services-in-this-country grid, China-vs-Mexico comparison table where useful, quote.
Geography spoke 18 The "we do X service in Y country" page (e.g., /mexico/die-casting/). Hero, country-specific context (USMCA tariff-free, lead time advantage, QC), lead-time table, link back to country hub + service hub, quote.

How to choose a template. Look at the URL. /services/X/ = service hub. /services/X/Y/ = service spoke. /X/ where X is a country = geo hub. /X/Y/ where X is a country = geo spoke. /resources/X-vs-Y/ = resource comparison. Anything else = utility (one-off bespoke layout).

Section 12

The quote form, the conversion engine

The quote form is the primary conversion surface. It lives at /quote/ as a dedicated page and as a sticky right-rail on every service hub, spoke, and geo page. Multi-step. CAD upload required. Filters for $50K+ implicitly via the volume slider.

Steps in plain English

  1. Service. What service do you need? Pre-filled from query param if buyer arrived from a specific service or geo page (e.g. ?service=aluminum-die-casting).
  2. Material + tolerance. What material? What tolerance? Material dropdown is scoped to the selected service (zinc options for zinc die casting, etc.).
  3. Volume. How many units per year? Slider 1 to 100,000+. This is the implicit $50K filter. Sub-floor volumes go into a templated nurture, not sales priority.
  4. Lead time. Standard, expedited, or flexible. If "expedited" + China is the implied geo, surface Mexico USMCA alternative inline.
  5. CAD upload. STEP, IGES, DWG, max 50MB. Goes to Vercel Blob, virus-scanned, attached to HubSpot record.
  6. Contact details. Name, email, company, phone. Email validation. ReCAPTCHA v3.

Definition of done, per page

  • Trust bar (ISO 9001 + IATF 16949 + Since 2018) above the fold on service + geo pages
  • H1 word-for-word matches the primary keyword (or its closest natural variant)
  • Quote form embedded (sticky right-rail or inline)
  • Zero banned words ("factories," "global," "sourcing company," "middleman," "engineering" in service context, "made in USA," bare "prototype" without production framing)
  • Zero em dashes
  • No firearms imagery
  • $50K minimum implicit in copy + form filtering
  • City-level specificity where geo applies (Monterrey, Shanghai, Mumbai)
  • Halliburton case study or relevant proof linked from service pages
  • FAQ schema on service hubs and comparison pages (3-6 Q&A)
  • BreadcrumbList schema on every page
  • Lighthouse SEO ≥ 95, Accessibility ≥ 95, performance hard floors all green
  • InternalLinkBlock auto-injected with related spokes / geo / resources
  • Quote form CTA more prominent than any secondary CTA
  • Customer logos in COLOR (not B&W), Amazon larger
  • Page-specific background photo (no reusing wrong-service hero images)
  • Mobile renders cleanly at 375px wide
