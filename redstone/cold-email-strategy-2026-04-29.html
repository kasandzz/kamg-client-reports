What was decided on the April 28 call

The 39-minute Zoom call ended early when the connection dropped. Kas continued recording on his phone for the final three minutes, capturing five additional decisions Eric and Alec made before the call closed. Both segments are reflected below.

Topic Decision
Industries IN Firearms, robotics, industrial machinery, off-road vehicles and accessories, dental equipment, fitness equipment, defense (non-aerospace), electrical and electrical manufacturing, industrial automation, machinery, mechanical and industrial engineering. Underlying pattern: smart-device companies that assemble in the United States.
Industries OUT Aerospace and medical removed. No track record in either, no proof points to lead with.
Geography United States primary, Canada acceptable. Mexico explicitly excluded since Mexico is supply, not a buyer market.
Company size Track A and most volume tracks: 5 to 200 employees. Track B (China-exit angle): 200 to 5,000 employees. Track F (reps): under 10 employees. Filter on employee count, not revenue, since revenue figures in Apollo are projected and unreliable.
Job titles Director of Engineering, Engineering Manager, Engineering Supervisor, Senior and Lead Engineer, Director of Manufacturing, Director of Operations, Plant Manager, Supply Chain Manager, Director and VP Supply Chain, Procurement Manager, Director of Procurement. Anyone in supply chain at any level plus high-level engineering. Excluded: consultants, distributors, importers, sourcing agents, traders, brokers.
Campaign architecture One domain per angle. Each domain runs through a roughly 1,000-lead list at its own ramp pace, exhausting in approximately two months. Schedule and order tracked in a simple sheet. Roughly three tests live at any given time.
Tariff angle New Section 301 tariffs going up summer 2026. Plant the seed now via cold email so prospects remember Redstone the moment the tariff hits. No meeting CTA in the first email, just "wanted to be on your radar before summer."
Monthly nurture New For contacts who received the full three-touch sequence and did not reply, bounce, or unsubscribe: a personalized monthly email (not a newsletter, not a blast) every four to six weeks. Skip months where there is no real signal.
Reply handling Positive replies sync to HubSpot with full enriched data (website, company size, industry, original opener body). Zapier auto-forwards positives to Alec's inbox so he replies natively from his usual address. A new Slack channel, #redstone-cold-email-replies, pings Alec on his phone for every neutral or positive reply. Negatives are silently suppressed.
Industry case-study table Two columns. Column 1: parts Redstone has already produced for that industry. Column 2: parts not yet produced but a strong fit. Eric explicitly added column 2 at the close of the call so we can pitch into adjacent verticals where capability exists but track record does not.
Approval gate Kas reviews any team change before it goes live, including homepage updates. Eric raised this directly: "I want you to be the person that checks it rather than me."
HubSpot lead-form gap Rachel surfaced that the past five days of leads did not trigger email notifications. Likely scope: only the new slide-out form on AdWords landing pages. Standard HubSpot form fills on /contact and /quote should be unaffected. Test pass and report due Wednesday April 30 EOD.

Section 4

Suppression list, built before any send

Hitting an existing customer or active deal with a cold email burns trust faster than any other failure mode. The suppression list is built before launch and imported to Lemlist as a global blocklist that applies across every campaign and every domain.

Pulled from HubSpot
Existing relationships
  • All companies tagged "Customer" (current and past)
  • All contacts on any open deal, regardless of pipeline stage
  • All contacts with lifecycle stage "Customer"
  • All contacts with HubSpot activity in the past 90 days from form fills, website, or sales-led channels
  • Anyone tagged "Do Not Contact" (property to be created if it does not exist)
  • Internal team: anyone @redstonemanufacturing.com plus Eric and Alec's personal addresses
Manually added
Direct competitor domains
  • xometry.com, fictiv.com, protolabs.com, hubs.com, 3erp.com
  • rapiddirect.com, jiga.io, get3dsmith.com, pcbway.com
  • icomold.com, star-rapid.com
  • Eric and Alec to confirm any additions
Manually added
Aggregator and broker domains
  • thomasnet.com
  • mfg.com
  • kompass.com
  • europages.com
Domain class blocks
Non-buyer domains
  • *.gov, *.mil, *.edu suppressed entirely
  • Free-mail providers (gmail, yahoo, outlook, hotmail, aol, icloud, proton) suppressed at campaign filter for Tracks A through E
  • Track F whitelists indie reps using Gmail manually
Validation pass
Email validation pre-Lemlist
  • Apollo built-in verifier, verified status only
  • NeverBounce or MillionVerifier second-pass for any list over 2,000 contacts
  • Drop deliverability scores under 85 percent
  • Drop catch-all domains entirely
Lemlist global rules
Ongoing automatic suppression
  • Any "unsubscribe" or "remove me" reply auto-suppresses
  • Any hard bounce auto-suppresses
  • Any spam classification auto-suppresses across all campaigns
  • Any positive reply auto-suppresses from all other active campaigns so a hot lead never gets multi-touched from three angles

Section 6

Reply handling: how a positive reply moves through the system

Designed to keep Alec out of new tools as much as possible. He answers the question "did this person reply?" from his phone or his existing inbox.

Lemlist sentiment HubSpot action Notification
Positive Create deal at "Email Reply" stage. Tag "Source = Cold Email". Sync the full enriched contact record (website, company size, industry, original opener body). Assign to Alec. Slack DM Alec, plus email forward via Zapier so Alec can reply natively from his usual address.
Neutral Add contact to HubSpot list "Cold Email — Neutral Replies." Do not create a deal. Slack DM Alec.
Negative Suppress globally in Lemlist. No HubSpot record. Silent.
Out-of-office Pause sequence for that contact 7 days. No HubSpot record. Silent.
Auto-responder (other) Pause sequence. No HubSpot record. Silent.
The reply-from-different-domain workaround. Each campaign sends from a different domain, so Alec replying from his usual address would normally break the email chain. The Zapier rule auto-forwards every Lemlist positive reply to alec@redstonemanufacturing.com. Alec opens the forward, deletes the auto-generated forward header (the "Forwarded message" block plus the timestamp lines above it), and replies in his own client. The thread chains correctly and Lemlist marks the contact engaged. A one-pager will document this for Alec on launch day.
The Slack channel for Alec. A new channel #redstone-cold-email-replies in the team Slack, with Alec invited as a guest user via Google SSO. Lemlist webhooks post into the channel: positive replies tag Alec so his phone gets the push notification, neutral replies post silently for visibility, negatives are not posted because they are already suppressed. Alec can acknowledge from his phone without ever opening Slack desktop.

Section 8

Risk register

Every risk below has a documented mitigation. None of these are theoretical. They are the failure modes I have personally seen burn cold email programs.

Risk Mitigation
Hit existing customer with cold email Suppression master list mandatory. Nothing sends until it is built and imported to Lemlist global blocklist.
Domain burn from too-fast ramp 10 to 30 emails per inbox per day with weekly +5 increments. Hard halt rule: any inbox over 5 percent bounce in 48 hours pauses immediately and restarts warmup. Domain stays live unless 2 of 3 inboxes on it breach.
Spam trigger words in copy Banned word list enforced. Pre-send linter on every sequence checks for banned phrases before activation.
Negative reply leaks into HubSpot pipeline Lemlist sentiment classifier gates HubSpot writes. Negatives suppress only, no record created.
No visibility for Eric for a week Mon and Thu tracker non-negotiable. Real numbers, no projections, regardless of how the first week looks.
Track F consumes all team time at expense of A and B Track F capped at 4 hours per week of team bandwidth. Tracks A and B get the remaining 80 percent.
Apollo list 30 percent or more junk Spot-check 25 random rows before push. Refine and re-pull if junk rate is high. Validate before any sequence is activated against the list.
Warmup decay during the 27 idle days since infra was built Each inbox validated at 5 to 10 emails per day for 5 days before the ramp begins. Any inbox below 75 percent reputation in Lemlist warmup is held off the live send and continues warming.
