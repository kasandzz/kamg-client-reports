The Two Competitors

Who They Are, Side By Side

Fictiv and Xometry both run aggressive Google Ads in the manufacturing category but with very different ICPs and economics. Understanding the difference is what tells Redstone where to fight and where to stay out of the auction.

Public Marketplace

Xometry (NASDAQ: XMTR)

  • 2025 revenue: $686.6M, +25.87 percent YoY
  • Active buyers: 81,821. Active suppliers: 4,996
  • International revenue: $113M, +27 percent (18 percent of marketplace)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: $18.5M positive (vs -$9.7M loss in 2024)
  • Estimated paid search spend: $2.5M to $5M per month (inferred from 25 to 30 percent S&M of revenue norm)
  • Bidding posture: wide and shallow, every process and every material
  • Hero hook: "Sub plus or minus 0.001 inch Accuracy, Zero Guesswork"
  • Cert stack: ISO 9001, IATF 16949, ISO 13485, AS9100D, ITAR, CMMC L2, JCP
Private, MISUMI Subsidiary

Fictiv (acquired April 2025)

  • Total raised pre-acquisition: $192M over 7 rounds
  • Acquired by MISUMI Group: $350M cash, April 17, 2025
  • Now owns meviy USA (transitioned April 17, 2026)
  • Headcount: ~400. Network: ~250 partners
  • Customers: 5,600 plus. Parts manufactured: 39M plus
  • Estimated paid search: $500K to $1.5M per month (private, unverifiable)
  • ICP: prototype to mid-volume, tight-tolerance CNC plus injection molding plus 3DP. Now bids die casting and sheet metal too.
  • Hero hook: "Custom mechanical parts as fast as 1 day"
The spend asymmetry that matters. Redstone is at $5K per month. Xometry is at an estimated $2.5M to $5M per month. That is a 500x to 1000x gap. We cannot win generic head terms like "cnc machining services" or "instant quote cnc" no matter how good the copy is. The whole strategy is based on this fact: Redstone wins on long tail, country-specific, production-volume, and certification-led queries where the giants do not bother to message-match.

The Pattern Underneath

Steal These

  • One number, headlined, with the standard. Xometry's "Sub ±0.001 inch" is durable because the spec is the headline. Redstone's ±0.0005 inch belongs in every CNC headline, paired with ISO 2768-fH or GD&T 1.1.
  • Three hard numbers above the fold. Fictiv stacks "1 day," "95.4 percent on-time," "±0.0001 inch." Three numbers, no adjectives. Replace any Redstone hero adjective stack with three hard numbers: tolerance, on-time rate, expedite window.
  • CTA split by complexity. Fictiv uses "Get Instant Quote" on prototype services and "Submit RFQ" on production. Redstone is RFQ-only, this matches.
  • Tariff acknowledgment as a wedge. Xometry's defensive tariff line is Redstone's offensive USMCA hook.
  • Verbatim engineer quote, named. Fictiv's sheet metal LP cites "Joe Tedesco, Lead Aerospace Engineer, Heliospace." A logo wall says "this brand exists." A named engineer says "an engineer like you uses this."
  • Comparison LP for bottom-of-funnel queries. /xometry-alternative and /fictiv-alternative pages convert at 3 to 5x informational pages.

Do Not Copy These

  • "Instant quote" CTAs on production pages. Pulls prototype-stage buyers Redstone explicitly does not want. Banned phrase per positioning. Use "Submit RFQ" or "Get Custom Quote."
  • "As fast as 1 day" / "as fast as 24 hours." Prototype shop promises. Redstone's lane is 4 to 8 week standard, 2 week expedite. Promising 1 day positions Redstone as a Fictiv-lite competitor on Fictiv's terms.
  • "Vetted global network" or "5,000 plus suppliers." Broker confession. Redstone's structural advantage is the opposite: named foundries, owned relationships, single US account manager.
  • "No Minimum Order Quantity." Fictiv prototype positioning. Redstone's $50K plus MOV is the qualifier. State it positively: "production runs starting at $50K."
  • Logo walls of consumer brands (Honeywell, Meta, Google, Ford, Lyft, NASA, DoorDash). Redstone does not have these and chasing them dilutes positioning. Industrial OEM tier-2/3 named-engineer quotes win the actual procurement decision.
  • "Free Casting Samples For Approval Before Production." This is Redstone's current headline. It loses to Xometry's $1,000 promo on cold paid traffic. Replace with production-credibility copy.

Landing Page Strategy

Process-Named vs Buyer-Named LPs

Xometry's LP architecture is process-named (CNC, die casting, sheet metal). Fictiv's is the same. Both are designed to convert any-volume any-country click into the instant-quote engine and let the algorithm route. That works at marketplace scale. It loses to a buyer-named LP for narrow procurement intent.

LP Architecture Choice Xometry / Fictiv Redstone
LP naming convention Process-named (e.g., /capabilities/cnc-machining-service) Buyer-named (e.g., /die-casting-mexico, /cnc-machining-mexico, /aluminum-die-casting-monterrey)
CTA on production pages Fictiv uses RFQ form ("Submit Die Casting RFQ Now"). Xometry stays "Get Instant Quote" everywhere. RFQ form on every primary LP. Redstone's $50K MOV ICP is not an instant-quote market.
Country naming in hero None. Both default to "global network." Country and city in H1. "Precision Die Casting in Monterrey, Mexico."
Cert placement Fictiv hides AS9100D / IATF / ISO 13485 on /production only. Homepage and prototype LPs show only ISO 9001. IATF 16949 in the hero of every production LP. ISO 9001 is now table-stakes, do not lead with it alone.
Customer proof Logo walls of consumer brands (Honeywell, Meta, Google, NASA, DoorDash). Named-engineer verbatim quotes from industrial OEM tier-2/3 accounts. Pull 2 to 3 quotes per LP.
Lead-time framing "As fast as 1 day" (prototype). "T1 samples 10 to 15 days" (die casting). "4 to 8 week standard, 2 week expedite" framed as production-grade, not prototype-grade.
Tolerance framing Hero: "±0.0001 inch" (Fictiv), "±0.001 inch" (Xometry CNC). Spec table elsewhere. Hero: ±0.0005 inch with the standard (ISO 2768-fH). Spec table directly under hero.
Comparison LPs Fictiv has /xometry-competitor-alternative. Xometry has none. Build /xometry-alternative and /fictiv-alternative. Captures bottom-of-funnel comparison searchers.
The architecture pattern, simply. A buyer who searches "die casting mexico monterrey" and lands on Xometry's generic /die-casting-services page sees nothing about Mexico in the hero. Quality Score for that auction would be poor for Xometry, message-match would fail. A Redstone LP titled "Precision Die Casting in Monterrey, Mexico, IATF 16949" wins that auction at lower CPC, with higher CTR, every time.
