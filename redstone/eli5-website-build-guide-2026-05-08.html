How to use this guide read order matters

The team using this brief: KAMG (Kas, Leo, Rehan), Outrider (Jack and team), and the design/wireframe lead. Goal of this document: get every wireframe drafted from the same understanding of intent, voice, and Eric's rules.

  1. Read Eric's voice rules and the above-fold mandate first. These bind every page. If a wireframe violates them, the wireframe is wrong, not the rule.
  2. Match the page to one of 6 templates. Every page in the sitemap is tagged with a template number. Pull the template spec, then layer the page-specific notes.
  3. Read the page-level ELI5 + Eric notes. Tells you who the user is, what they came to do, and which language Eric will or will not approve.
  4. Check the wireframe hint. Section order, above-fold mandates, CTA progression. Treat as the skeleton, not the design.
  5. If anything in this brief conflicts with Jack's research (linked at the bottom), surface it before wireframing. We resolved the conflicts we caught, but new ones will appear during build.

Eric's voice rules always-on, every page, no exceptions

Eric is an engineer. He evaluates copy the way an engineer reads a spec sheet: numbers before adjectives, proof before claim, certs before story. This voice profile was extracted from the April 9 Fathom call where he approved or rejected every line on the homepage live. Treat it as a hard contract.

Tone DOs

  • Engineer to engineer. Short sentences. One idea per line. Average 12-16 words.
  • Lead with a number or a cert. ISO 9001, IATF 16949, since 2018, 2,000+ programs, 4-8 wk, ±0.0005".
  • Spec first, claim second. "ISO-certified facilities. Done." beats "World-class quality."
  • Cautiously under-promised. Say 20%, not 30%. Say "consistently short," not "industry-leading."
  • City-specific. "Monterrey" beats "Mexico." "São Paulo" beats "Brazil." "Shanghai" beats "Asia."
  • Progressive CTA ladder, top to bottom: Request Quote → Start My Project → Move Into Production.

Tone DON'Ts

  • No superlatives. world-class, best-in-class, premier, industry-leading. Eric will reject on sight.
  • No "Global." Eric struck this from the homepage personally.
  • No "Factories." Use Supplier, Manufacturer, Foundry. Eric: "I don't want to seem like a sourcing company."
  • No "Precision Engineering." Eric: "we don't do engineering."
  • No tariff-led headlines. Eric said "No" to leading with tariff angle. Tariff-inclusive estimates as a trust block deeper down is fine.
  • No "Build it" as a CTA. Eric: "people don't build with our parts, they start production."
  • No infographics, no spirals, no "flashy" treatments. Tables and grid only.
Banned phrases hard list. Hardcoded. Treat as build-blockers if any wireframe copy lands on these words: Global · Factory / Factories · Precision Engineering · Seattle (we are Ohio-HQ'd) · nearshore / nearshoring · made in USA CNC · US-based die casting · domestic manufacturer · middleman · marketplace · international factories · sourcing company · Build it (CTA) · Talk to a mechanical engineer (deep-page CTA) · world-class · best-in-class · premier · industry-leading.
Approved hook (use everywhere we need a one-liner): "The trustworthiness of an American company with the pricing from overseas." Rachel Gill drafted, Eric approved. Anchor hook for hero pull-quote, email P.S., meta description, social cards.

The 6 page templates cover all 71 pages

Six reusable templates power the entire site. Every page in the sitemap below is tagged with a template number. Wireframe each template once, then populate per-page content. Build time goes from 71 unique designs to 6 templates + 71 fills.

Template 01

Homepage

ELI5: The shop window. A buyer who has never heard of Redstone lands here, and in 5 seconds decides whether we are credible enough to ask for a quote. Goal is to convert "ad click" or "branded search" into "quote request."

  • Hero: H1 = "Custom Metal Fabrication" (or Eric-approved variant), sub = approved geo + POC line
  • Trust bar: ISO + IATF + Since 2018 + 2,000+ programs
  • Customer logo strip (whitelisted: CIRCOR, Honda, MIT, MEYER, Mahindra, Amazon, GameStop)
  • 4 capability cards (die casting, CNC, sheet metal, Mexico) with spec tables, not adjectives
  • Halliburton case study pull-quote ($1.2M savings, 50 foundries evaluated)
  • Process strip: Same-day feasibility → 4-8 wk lead → 2-wk expedite
  • Sticky quote CTA, footer with all 4 country addresses (Cam's SEO intent)
Used by: 1 page · /
Template 02

Geography hub

ELI5: A buyer who already knows they want manufacturing in a specific country (Mexico, India, China, Brazil) lands here. The page proves we have a real operation in that country, then routes them to the specific service they need.

  • Hero: H1 = "[Service] in [Country]" or "[Country] Foundries" depending on anchor keyword
  • Country-specific city anchor: Monterrey, Mumbai, Shanghai, São Paulo
  • Trust bar (always-on)
  • Country differentiator block: Mexico = USMCA + lead time; India = scale + cost; China = QC oversight; Brazil = sand-casting depth
  • 5-8 spoke service cards linking to /[country]/[service]/ pages
  • Case study or proof point if available
  • Quote form embed
Used by: 4 pages · /mexico/, /india/, /china/, /brazil/
Template 03

Geography spoke (geo + service)

ELI5: The most specific buyer. They typed "die casting Mexico" or "cnc machining india." They already know what process and where. The page must answer: yes we do that there, here is the proof, here is the quote form. Highest commercial intent in the entire site.

  • Hero: H1 mirrors keyword exactly ("Die Casting in Mexico" not "Mexico's Premier Die Casting")
  • Trust bar (always-on)
  • Country + service capability table: tolerances, materials, volume, lead time
  • Country-specific advantage: Mexico = nearshore cost + USMCA; China = QC + tariff context (not headline); India = scale; Brazil = sand-casting heritage
  • FAQ schema block (3-5 Q&A) for AI Overview citation
  • Quote form embed with CAD upload
  • Cross-link to parent geo hub + service hub
Used by: 18 pages · all /[country]/[service]/
Template 04

Service hub

ELI5: The buyer knows they need die casting, or CNC, or sheet metal, but not yet which material or geography. The page is a category landing: it covers the service end-to-end, then sends them deeper to a material or country spoke. Commercial intent, RFQ-driven.

  • Hero: H1 = "[Service] Services" matching ad-group intent
  • Trust bar (always-on)
  • Process explainer (4-6 steps) with numbers (lead time, tolerance, capacity)
  • Material spoke grid (links to /services/[service]/[material]/ pages)
  • FAQ schema block (5-8 Q&A) for AI Overview citation
  • Halliburton or relevant case study pull-quote
  • Quote form embed
Used by: 11 pages · /services/[service]/
Template 05

Service spoke (material or process variant)

ELI5: The narrowest service search. "Aluminum die casting." "High pressure die casting." "Cold chamber die casting." Buyer knows the exact spec they need. Page proves we run that material or process at production volume.

  • Hero: H1 mirrors keyword exactly
  • Trust bar (always-on)
  • Material or process spec table: alloys supported, tolerance ranges, finish options, MOQ, lead time
  • Use cases: 3-5 industry applications with part type examples
  • FAQ schema (3-5 Q&A)
  • Cross-link to parent service hub + relevant comparison page (e.g., aluminum-vs-zinc)
  • Quote form embed
Used by: 17 pages · /services/[service]/[material]/
Template 06

Resource (comparison or guide)

ELI5: The buyer has not yet decided. They are searching "forged vs cast" or "sg iron vs cast iron" because they are spec'ing a part. Page job is to be the trusted decision-support resource Google cites in AI Overviews, then quietly route the reader down to the relevant service page. Top-of-funnel education that earns commercial intent.

  • Hero: H1 mirrors comparison keyword exactly ("Forged vs Cast" not "Which Process Is Right")
  • TL;DR table: 3-5 row at-a-glance comparison (cost, strength, lead time, finish, MOQ)
  • Decision guide: when to choose A, when to choose B, with use case examples
  • FAQ schema block (5-8 Q&A) — primary AI Overview defense
  • Cost/tradeoff drivers explained inline (no calculator unless we build one)
  • Routes to relevant service hub + spoke at bottom (not in hero)
  • Quote form embed at footer only (informational intent, do not interrupt early)
Used by: 21 pages · /resources/* + comparison cluster
Conditional 7th template, only if certs land. Industry pages (/industries/automotive/, /industries/aerospace/) take a hybrid Template 04 + 06 layout: hero mirrors industry keyword, cert badge prominent in trust bar, regulated-program proof points, 3-5 part type examples, FAQ schema, quote form. Build only if Eric confirms IATF 16949 and AS9100.
