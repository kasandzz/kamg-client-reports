05  Emergent threads

Six new threads opened today (none on Tuesday's prep)

1. SEO collapse blame — pre-Sep 2025 cause hunt

Eric reframed the whole SEO conversation today. Previously the narrative was "Adam (prior SEO) made changes Sep–Nov 2025 that crashed rankings." Today Eric escalated: "crashed our SEO after [August 2025]."

The shift from "after Adam's changes" to "after August 2025" is meaningful. It suggests Eric is no longer sure when or how the collapse happened — just that it did. This anxiety reads as: he's been told too many theories, and he's looking for a deterministic fix (revert everything).

Counter-narrative we can deploy: Reverting site to Aug 2025 deletes 8 months of work and doesn't restore the rankings — rankings collapsed because of off-page factors (China supplier copying site, link decay, content drift) plus a few on-page issues that we already fixed. The path forward is forward, not backward.

2. Backup-as-insurance — trust signal

Eric requesting a backup of the current site before Jack's new build is deployed is rational. But it's also a tell: he's hedging on Jack already, on day 1 of onboarding.

Two interpretations:

  • Charitable: Eric is a careful operator. Backups are basic hygiene.
  • Less charitable: Eric just paid a new SEO vendor, and within 24 hours of Jack's first email he's asking for an exit hatch.

Implication: Jack's first deliverable has to land cleanly. Kas and Leo own Jack's onboarding to Eric — treat it as a high-stakes referral, not a side quest.

3. Aluminum-only die casting — strategic narrowing

"95% of our die casting leads want aluminum" is a major data point Eric has not surfaced before. It changes everything downstream:

  • SEO LP: /die-casting-seo/ becomes /aluminum-die-casting/ (or anchored on aluminum H1).
  • Existing LPs: die-casting-services-mexico, die-casting-services-india — do we re-anchor on aluminum or maintain die-casting-as-umbrella?
  • Keyword research: validate aluminum die casting volume vs generic die casting.
  • Quote form: aluminum option already there? Default selection?
  • Industries hub: aluminum-heavy industries (auto, EV, off-road, fitness) become primary illustrations.

Action: Pull lead history (BQ lp_leads + HubSpot) and confirm 95% aluminum claim before reshaping the entire site.

4. Pressure die casting keyword question

Eric: "the process is more commonly called high pressure die casting or just die casting. Did keyword research suggest using pressure die casting?"

This is Eric checking our work. He's right — "high pressure die casting" is the proper industry term; "pressure die casting" is unusual. Either:

  • Our keyword research did show meaningful volume on "pressure die casting" (UK term, or specific niche) — we need to show him the data.
  • Or the keyword choice was made without verifying volume — we update to "high pressure die casting" or just "die casting."

Action: Pull SEMrush / Ahrefs / GSC data on the three variants. Reply to Eric with the table. Update LPs to whichever wins.

5. Hosting.com email-takeover attempt — vendor capture risk

Hosting.com replied to Rehan's investigation by suggesting Eric switch all email TO Hosting.com. Eric correctly resisted. Kas backed him: "Definitely not. Google is the best email provider."

What this surfaces: Hosting.com sees an opening because the form-notification incident exposes that something email-related is broken. The actual fix is DKIM authentication on Google Workspace. Until DKIM lands cleanly, Hosting.com can keep poking.

Action: Lock DKIM authentication this week. Eric must do the steps (Workspace admin), Rehan must add the record, then click "start authenticating." 24-48h for DNS propagation.

6. Domain inventory — redstone-manufacturing.com (hyphen)

Leo's audit surfaced Eric is paying for two domains that look duplicative:

  • redstonemanufacturing.com — primary, 3-year renewal just charged ($$$).
  • redstone-manufacturing.com — with hyphen, Eric confirmed he recognizes it.

SEO question: Is the hyphenated domain serving content, redirecting, or just sitting? If it's serving duplicate content of the primary, that's a cannibalization issue Google would penalize. If it redirects to primary via 301, it's harmless and protective. If it's parked, no risk.

Action: WHOIS the hyphenated domain, check NS records, check live HTTP response, document state. If duplicate-serving, set up a 301 to primary same day.
