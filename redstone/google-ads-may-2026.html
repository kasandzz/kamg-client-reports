The decision on Search 2026

Two readings of the same numbers

There is a case for relaunching the paused main campaign and a case for waiting. Both are legitimate readings of the same data. The difference comes down to which conversion definition you use and which time horizon you weight more heavily.

View A: Relaunch

The case to relaunch

  • Negative keyword list grew from 3,409 to 8,503 over the campaign's life (+5,094 negatives added). The campaign was filtering wasted searches at an accelerating rate.
  • The ad groups still live at the moment of pause had reached $164 per conversion, well below the $300 account-wide average.
  • Account-wide CTR finished at 4.58%, above Google's 2% "good" threshold. Messaging was resonating with the people who saw the ads.
  • Prior comparable spend windows produced materially worse results than this 30-day period (0 leads on similar spend in earlier 2024 cycles).
  • Search campaigns of this structure typically need 2 to 3 months of buildup before they settle. Pausing at the end of month 3 throws away the investment in months 1 and 2.
View B: Hold or restructure first

The case to wait

  • Within Search 2026 alone, the CPL is $506 ($2,024 spend, 4 conversions). If only sales-ready conversions are counted (2 of those 4), CPL rises to $1,012.
  • The two highest-spend ad groups in Search 2026 ("Custom Sheet Metal Fabrication" at $454 and "Precision Machining Services" at $207) did not record an associated conversion in the keyword-level data. The "performing ad groups at pause" framing holds at the aggregate but breaks down at the keyword level.
  • The conversion tracking flags (Submit lead form, Sign-up, Page view) mean the headline lead numbers carry uncertainty. Resolve those before relying on them.
  • If the original reason to pause was cash protection, the underlying constraint has not shifted because Google's CTR is good. The relaunch decision is a cash question first and a Google Ads question second.

Both views work from the same data. We have presented them side by side rather than picking one because the deciding factor (your closed-loop sales economics and cash position) lives outside the Google Ads platform.

What we recommend doing first

Five moves before the pause / unpause call

We are not making the pause / unpause call in this report. That call rests partly on closed-loop sales economics and cash flow, both of which live outside the Google Ads platform. What we recommend doing first, regardless of which way you eventually go:

  1. Resolve the conversion tracking flags

    Fix the "Submit lead form", "Sign-up", and "Page view" goal configurations. This costs nothing and removes a key source of doubt about the lead count.

  2. Audit the Brand campaign conversion path

    Why are people who search "Redstone Manufacturing" by name not converting? Check the landing page they reach, the form route, and the time-to-conversion. This is the single highest-intent traffic the account receives.

  3. Run a lead-quality back-check with your sales team

    Of the 6 sales-ready leads from the last 30 days (3 quote requests, 1 LP form submission, 2 phone / mail contacts), how many became actual conversations and how many advanced to quote stage? That gives you the closed-loop CPA, which is the number that matters most for the relaunch decision.

  4. Use the Location Specific budget that is sitting unspent

    The active campaign with the lowest CPL is leaving roughly $130 per day of its budget unused. Closing that gap is a higher-leverage move than relaunching Search 2026 today.

  5. Then revisit the Search 2026 decision

    Once steps 1 through 4 are done, the real CPA is known and the relaunch can be evaluated against actual economics rather than platform-reported CPL. The two views above will collapse into one answer once the underlying data is trusted.
