01
Top priority for Month 2

Google's tracking has been letting us down — the 6 leads figure is a floor, not the real number.

Every conversion pixel inside Google Ads has been flagging "needs attention" since launch. In plain English: the wires that tell Google "a quote was requested" haven't been firing cleanly, so the platform's own count can't be trusted as the source of truth.

The 6 leads figure above is what Google is currently willing to confirm — 3 brochure downloads and 3 quote requests it could actually tie back to an ad click. Your real number is almost certainly higher. Anything Google couldn't reliably attribute to a click simply didn't get counted.

What we're doing: rebuilding the tracking from scratch on a more reliable stack (PostHog + server-side events) so every quote request and brochure download is captured cleanly and fed back into Google Ads for optimisation. Targeted live by 24 April. Once it's in, Month 2's true cost-per-lead becomes visible inside a week — and we'll be able to validate those leads against your CRM once one's in place.

02

10,164 changes — almost all of them adding "do not show ads for this" rules.

The biggest job in Month 1 was teaching Google what not to show your ads for. We grew your negative-keyword list from 3,409 → 7,044 — that's 3,635 new "do not show" rules added in 31 days.

Why so many? Google has changed how keyword matching works. Even when we tell it "only show ads for 'precision CNC machining Mexico'," it now also shows ads for vaguely related searches like 'machine shop hobby' or 'CNC for sale' — people who will never buy from Redstone. Our job is to spot those wasted impressions every day and block them one by one.

It's tedious work, but it's the only way to clean up the traffic. And it's already working — which leads to the next number.

03
Trending up

Click-through rate jumped 34% in 4 weeks, despite Easter.

Click-through rate (CTR) is the cleanest signal that traffic quality is improving. The more relevant the people seeing your ads, the more of them click. Here's the trajectory:

4.42%

Easter is the second-worst week of the year for Google Ads (after Christmas) — B2B traffic always drops. So the raw 4.42% in Easter week is actually equivalent to ~5.23% in a normal week. Either way, the line is moving the right direction. Month 2 starts with all that negative-keyword groundwork already paid for.

04

India + Mexico now have their own campaign — they were getting starved.

When we launched, your India and Mexico location ad groups were sitting inside the main campaign — and getting zero impressions. The broader generic terms ("CNC manufacturing", "sheet metal fabrication") were eating the entire daily budget before the location ads got a chance to run.

We split them out into a separate campaign with their own dedicated budget. Now they actually run. The catch: location-targeted clicks cost more.

Location clicks are more than 2× the price. That's the cost of buyers who are specifically searching for "CNC machining Mexico" instead of just "CNC machining." They're more qualified — but if they land on a generic page, we waste the click. That's why dedicated India and Mexico landing pages are now a Month 2 priority (see Section 7).

05

We stopped showing your ads to 18–24 year olds.

The 18–24 age bracket was the worst-performing audience in the account by every metric we track. They click but they don't buy — they're not making sourcing decisions for industrial parts. Excluded mid-month. Frees up budget for the actual buyers (engineers and sourcing managers, typically 30–60).

Gaps we're closing in Month 2

Where the big players are beating us — and how we catch up.

Honest read: there are three things the incumbents do that Redstone doesn't yet. All three are on the Month 2 build list.

Service-specific landing pages
Protolabs has 200+ dedicated pages, one per service. Redstone currently sends every click to the homepage. Fix: the 7 service pages + 2 country pages already in build (see Section 7). Live by 17 April.
Instant-quote experience
Xometry's instant-quote engine is their moat. We won't replicate it in Month 2, but the new quote form at /quote-form-demo is a meaningful step up — faster, cleaner, and feeds directly into our lead tracker.
Ad extensions (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets)
Competitors run the full stack of extensions — worth an average 10–15% CTR lift. We're auditing Redstone's current extension setup this week and filling in what's missing.
Brand-defence campaign
If a competitor ever bids on "Redstone Manufacturing," there's currently no defence. Brand campaigns run at ~$0.50–$1.00 CPC and lock your own name. Proposing this for Month 3.
