08  Locked rules

Eric copy rules / banned phrases

Hardcoded from Apr 17, Apr 19, and Apr 28 calls. Apply to anything that lands in front of Eric — including SEO meta titles, descriptions, H1s, body copy, the deliverables tracker, and your Slack replies in #redstone-marketing.

Banned words / phrases

  • "project" / "projects" — use "parts"
  • "factories" — use "facility" (singular feel)
  • "marketplace" / "network" / "global network"
  • "made in USA" alone — use "U.S. manufacturer with Mexico / India / Taiwan facilities"
  • "3rd party / independent QC" — use "in-house inspection"
  • "ISO-certified company" — use "ISO-certified facilities" (only overseas hold ISO)
  • "AWS certified welders" (we don't)
  • "48-hour quotes"
  • "save money" — use "lower landed cost"
  • Section labeled "qualifications"

Geography / process

  • "Seattle" or "Ohio" in service LP copy
  • "5-axis" on machining LPs (we don't have it)
  • Em dashes in any client-facing copy (my permanent rule — AI tell)

Required framing

  • Lead with Mexico, then U.S. account manager
  • "Facility in Mexico" not "facilities"
  • ISO 9001 always, never just "ISO-certified"
  • Tolerance ±0.001"
  • IATF 16949 in body only, never in H1
  • USMCA tariff-free + 6–8 wk faster on every Mexico page
  • Red #B21E24 for CTAs only, never on fills
SEO-specific watchouts in this rule set:
  • "Parts" not "projects" — affects keyword targeting on "manufacturing projects"-class queries. We deliberately do NOT target those. Stay on parts / components / castings.
  • Single-facility feel — "facility in Mexico" is a content choice, not a literal claim.
  • ISO 9001 always — in title tags, meta descriptions, H1s. Never just "ISO-certified."
  • "Made in USA" gotcha — affects every meta description on the new build. Use the full phrasing or skip the claim.
