Submit Your Files
Upload your CAD files in STEP, IGES, DXF, or DWG format along with any 2D drawings. Our engineering team reviews every submission for manufacturability before quoting. We accept up to 10 files at 128MB each.

Receive a Quotation
Our engineers review your files and return a detailed quotation within 24-48 hours. The quote includes tooling costs, unit pricing by volume tier, lead time, and suggested material and finish options.
03
Tooling
Once approved, we design and manufacture your production tooling in-house. Tooling lead times are typically 4-6 weeks. You receive T1 sample parts with a full dimensional report before production is released.
04
Production
Production runs follow our documented quality control procedures at every stage. Your U.S.-based program manager provides weekly updates and photo documentation throughout the run.
05
Inspection and Delivery
All outgoing shipments include a CoC (Certificate of Conformance) and dimensional inspection report. We coordinate freight from our facilities directly to your dock, with full tracking provided.

Frequently Asked Questions

Standard lead time for die casting production is 8-12 weeks from approved tooling. Tooling itself takes approximately 4-6 weeks. Rush programs can be accommodated on a case-by-case basis.
We specialize in aluminum, zinc, and copper alloys for high-pressure die casting. Common alloys include A380, A360, ZA-8, ZA-12, and C85700. Our engineering team can recommend the best material for your application.
All Redstone facilities are ISO 9001 and IATF 16949 certified. We conduct in-process quality checks at every production stage and supplement internal inspection with third-party dimensional verification. Every shipment includes a Certificate of Conformance and full inspection documentation.
We require a minimum annual program value of $50,000. Volume minimums start at 500 units per production run. We are best suited for programs with consistent, recurring demand.
Every program is assigned a dedicated U.S.-based program manager who serves as your primary point of contact. They coordinate quoting, tooling, production scheduling, quality documentation, and logistics from our U.S. headquarters.
