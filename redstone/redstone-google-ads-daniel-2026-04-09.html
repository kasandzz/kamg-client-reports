Section 02

New Landing Page Structure

Each service page will follow the same structural template. The goal is to give engineers the information they need to self-qualify and submit a request without friction.

Element What We're Building Rationale
Headline Keyword-matched (e.g., "Custom Die Casting Services for Industrial Applications") Ad copy to headline message match is the single largest Quality Score factor. Also confirms to the visitor they landed in the right place.
Trust signals ISO 9001 and IATF 16949 certifications, prominently placed above the fold Redstone's strongest differentiators for procurement teams. Aerospace and automotive buyers filter on certs before anything else.
Technical specs Tolerances, materials, max dimensions, and surface finish options per service Engineers self-qualify without a phone call. Reduces unqualified leads. Increases contact-to-close rate.
Quote form Company, name, email, service dropdown, part quantity, timeline, and file upload (CAD/STEP/IGES) Matches the UX standard set by Xometry and Protolabs. File upload is a key differentiator for serious engineering buyers vs. casual inquiries.
Social proof Client logos and brief case studies where available Procurement and engineering teams want peer references. B2B buying cycles involve multiple stakeholders -- logos accelerate internal approvals.
Phone number Prominently displayed in header and near the form B2B buyers with complex requirements prefer a conversation before committing. Phone visibility increases qualified lead volume alongside form submissions.

Section 03

Competitive Context

For context only -- what the major players are doing in Google Ads and on their landing pages. No action items here. Purely background for strategic decisions when the time is right.

Competitor Google Ads Approach Landing Page Setup Conversion Mechanism
Xometry Massive keyword coverage across 5,000+ pages. Heavy remarketing investment. Aggressive on all manufacturing categories. Service-specific pages per process and material combination. Extremely granular -- separate pages for aluminum die casting vs. zinc die casting, etc. AI-powered instant quote. CAD upload required. No human interaction until quote is reviewed.
Protolabs Aggressive bidding on CNC machining, injection molding, and sheet metal. Primary CTA is "Get Instant Quote" throughout all ads. Dedicated pages per service with full spec sheets -- tolerances, 30+ materials, surface finishes, lead times. Engineers can validate fit before contacting. Automated quote with DFM (Design for Manufacturability) feedback. Instant pricing is the hook.
Fictiv Runs competitor conquest campaigns. "Xometry alternative" and "Protolabs alternative" pages for in-market buyers already researching. Quality-focused messaging. Case studies with brand-name clients (Honeywell, etc.) above the fold. Online quote with managed process. Positions as concierge vs. automated.
SendCutSend Performance marketing focus. 170+ materials as a core selling point in ad copy. Widely considered best UX in manufacturing. Parts Builder tool allows visitors to build and price before ever contacting. Instant pricing with CAD upload or template selection. Checkout-style flow -- no sales contact required for standard orders.
RapidDirect Aggressive Google Ads spend. Comparison blog content targeting buyers researching options (Xometry vs. RapidDirect, etc.). Free DFM feedback prominently featured as the lead offer. Lowers the barrier to first contact. Instant quote plus DFM review. Two conversion paths: quote-first and review-first.
Dynacast Moderate spend, brand-focused rather than keyword-aggressive. Less performance-oriented than the others listed. "Speak to an Engineer" CTA. More traditional B2B approach -- less automation, more relationship. Quote request form. No instant pricing. Longer sales cycle expected.

Section 04

Ideas to Consider

Patterns from the competitive research that might be worth exploring once the landing pages are live and tracking is clean. These are ideas for discussion -- not prescriptions. You'll know better than we will which are relevant given current campaign state.

Idea What It Is Potential Impact Notes
Remarketing Display ads targeting site visitors who didn't convert. Standard Google Display Network setup. Highest ROI campaign type for B2B -- stays top of mind during long procurement cycles. Estimated $300-500/mo budget. Makes more sense once service-specific LPs are live and conversion tracking is verified. No point running it against the current homepage.
Ad extensions Sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, and call extension additions to existing campaigns. CTR lift of 10-15% is typical. No additional cost per impression -- purely additive to existing spend. May already be set up in the account -- worth checking current state. If not, low-effort high-return addition.
Negative keyword list audit Periodic review of excluded terms as campaigns scale and new search queries appear. Keeps budget focused on buyer-intent traffic as query volume grows. Already in place per your build. Just flagging as a periodic maintenance item -- not a gap.
Nearshore / reshoring terms Keywords like "nearshore manufacturing," "reshoring from China," "manufacturing outsourcing Mexico." Very low competition, low CPC. Redstone's Mexico + US setup is a natural fit for reshoring narratives in current market conditions. Redstone already bids on "Mexico" variants. This expands into a broader reshoring and supply chain diversification narrative that's trending in industrial buying conversations.
Competitor conquest Bidding on "Xometry alternative," "Protolabs alternative," and similar comparison-intent keywords. High intent -- these buyers are already in-market and actively evaluating. Fictiv runs this approach with dedicated comparison pages. Requires a dedicated landing page making the case directly. Would need Eric's input on how aggressively to position vs. the major platforms.
US-capability keywords "Die casting USA," "CNC machining company USA," "American manufacturer die casting." Captures buyers with domestic-only requirements. Eric mentioned approximately 20% of prospects want US manufacturing per onboarding notes. Redstone's Seattle facility covers this. Current keyword set may not be explicitly targeting domestic-only intent -- could be a gap worth checking.
