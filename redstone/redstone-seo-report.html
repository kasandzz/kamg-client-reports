05 The Positioning Problem

This isn't just a branding issue. It's actively suppressing SEO performance.

Current Live Site

Sounds Like a Sourcing Company

"Enjoy the benefits of lower labor costs in markets like China, Mexico, India, Taiwan, and Brazil while capitalizing on the engineering expertise... that only a US-based company like Redstone Manufacturing can offer."

Buyers searching "manufacturer" don't want a middleman. This creates a mismatch between content and search intent.

Replit Draft (New Direction)

Positions as a Manufacturer

"Lower Landed Costs for Metal Parts / Castings | CNC Machining | Sheet Metal Fabrication. Manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Mexico, and Asia. One U.S. engineering team."

Aligns with target keywords: "die casting manufacturer," "CNC machining company." Google rewards intent-content match.

Counter-Strategy Recommended

Strategy 1

Target Commercial Intent

"Die casting manufacturer quote," "custom CNC machining services" -- buyers, not researchers. AI doesn't answer buying queries.

Strategy 2

Original Data AI Can't Replicate

"Mexico vs China die casting costs 2026" -- needs real project data. Google cites original research rather than absorbing it.

Strategy 3

Get Cited, Not Replaced

FAQ schema + concise answer format = AI Overviews cite Redstone as the source. Be the reference, not the victim.

Strategy 4

Build Linkable Assets

Total Landed Cost Calculator, tariff impact studies, Halliburton case study. Tools and data earn links that AI can't replace.
