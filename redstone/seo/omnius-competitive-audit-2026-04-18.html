The verdict: Omnius runs a masterclass in B2B content-led SEO. They've built 335+ indexed pages, own their niche keywords through aggressive topic clustering, and convert through social proof and gated resources, not forms-first. Their weakness is in technical SEO (missing meta descriptions, no SERP features, brand collision with omnius.com) and zero presence in broader category searches. The strategies that matter for Redstone are structural and content-architectural, not technical.

06 Conversion Design & Social Proof

How Omnius Converts Without a Pricing Page

No Pricing Page

Deliberately omitted. Forces prospects into conversation. Only CTAs are "Get Started," "Partner with us," "Book a call." This is pure consultative selling.

No Free Trial / Demo

No self-serve path. Every conversion goes through a human. This filters for serious buyers only. Pages that return 404: /pricing, /demo, /free-trial, /features.

No Contact Page (Traditional)

They have /contact-us but the primary conversion mechanism is modal forms embedded in CTAs throughout the site. Contact is distributed, not centralized.

08 Stealable Strategies for Redstone

23 Actionable Ideas - Prioritized by Impact

Each strategy is adapted from Omnius's SEO playbook to B2B manufacturing context. Sorted by expected impact on Redstone's organic traffic and lead generation.

TIER 1: Site Architecture (Build the Foundation)

01

Hub-and-Spoke Service Pages

CRITICAL Effort: HIGH

Create a dedicated landing page for each core service (die casting, CNC machining, metal stamping, injection molding, sheet metal fabrication) that serves as a hub. Each hub gets 5-10 supporting blog articles targeting long-tail keywords. Blog articles link back to the hub page.

Omnius example: /seo is the hub, supported by 40+ blog articles on SEO subtopics that all link back.
Redstone version: /die-casting is the hub, supported by articles like "aluminum vs zinc die casting," "die casting tolerances guide," "nearshore die casting advantages."

02

Industry-Specific Landing Pages

CRITICAL Effort: MEDIUM

Omnius has /saas-seo-agency, /fintech-seo, /llm-seo - each targeting a specific vertical. Redstone should have pages for each industry they serve: /automotive-manufacturing, /medical-device-casting, /aerospace-machining, /consumer-electronics-manufacturing.

Why it works: A procurement manager at a medical device company will click "medical device casting" over generic "die casting" every time. Industry pages rank for "[industry] + [service]" keywords with zero competition in manufacturing.

03

Fat Footer with Internal Link Network

HIGH Effort: LOW

Omnius has 50+ internal links in their footer organized into 4 columns. This is free internal link equity flowing to deep pages. Redstone should have a footer with: Services (5-8 links), Industries (5-8 links), Resources (guides, case studies), Company (about, contact, careers).

Quick win: Just reorganize the existing footer into a 4-column structure with 30-40 links. Every page on the site now passes PageRank to every important page.

04

Flat URL Structure (No Nesting)

HIGH Effort: LOW

Omnius uses /seo, /geo-agency, /saas-seo-agency - all one level deep. No /services/seo/enterprise. Flat URLs rank better and are easier for crawlers. Redstone should use: /die-casting, /cnc-machining, /automotive-manufacturing rather than /services/die-casting.

TIER 2: Content Strategy (Build the Moat)

05

Metrics-First Case Studies

CRITICAL Effort: MEDIUM

Omnius leads every case study with a quantified result: "0 to 2.7M visitors," "227.9% signup increase," "64% higher conversion." The metric IS the headline. Redstone should do the same: "47% Cost Reduction vs. Domestic Casting," "6-Week Prototype to Production," "0.003" Tolerance Achieved on Medical Components."

Format: Metric headline -> Client context (1 paragraph) -> Challenge -> Solution -> Results table -> CTA. Never bury the number.

06

Educational Blog Content (15+ Articles/Month Target)

HIGH Effort: HIGH

Omnius publishes ~15 articles/month. For manufacturing, Redstone doesn't need that volume but should target 4-8/month covering: material comparison guides, tolerance specifications, process selection guides, cost calculators, nearshoring advantages, quality certifications explained.

Content types to steal:
- "X vs Y" comparisons (aluminum vs zinc die casting)
- "How to" process guides (how to select a die casting partner)
- "Best practices" for engineers (design for manufacturability)
- Industry trend reports (nearshoring trends 2026)

07

Email-Gated Resource Guides

HIGH Effort: MEDIUM

Omnius gates their industry reports and comprehensive guides behind email capture. Redstone should create 3-5 gated resources: "Complete Guide to Nearshore Manufacturing" (PDF), "Die Casting Material Selection Handbook," "Cost Comparison Calculator: US vs. Mexico vs. China," "Quality Certification Checklist (IATF/ISO)."

08

Google Algorithm Updates Tracker (Adapted)

MEDIUM Effort: LOW

Omnius maintains a "Google Updates" page that drives recurring traffic from people checking algorithm changes. Redstone's version: a "Tariff & Trade Updates" or "Manufacturing Regulations Tracker" page. Updated monthly, tracks USMCA changes, tariff impacts, Section 301 updates. Engineers and procurement teams would bookmark this.

09

Comparison & "Best of" Content

MEDIUM Effort: MEDIUM

Omnius has articles like "Best AI SEO Agencies for SaaS" where they include themselves. Redstone should create: "Top 10 Die Casting Companies in North America," "Best Nearshore Manufacturing Partners 2026," "US vs Mexico vs China Manufacturing Comparison." Include Redstone in each list with an honest, not self-promotional tone.

Logo Bar Above the Fold

Named Testimonials with Specific Metrics

HIGH Effort: MEDIUM

Every Omnius testimonial has: full name, title, company, and a specific metric ("64% higher conversion rate"). Generic testimonials are invisible. Redstone needs: "John Smith, VP Engineering, [Company] - 'Redstone reduced our per-unit cost by 38% while maintaining IATF 16949 compliance.'"

12

Distributed CTAs (Not Just Contact Page)

HIGH Effort: LOW

Omnius has 3-4 CTA variants spread throughout every page: "Get Started," "Partner with us," "Book a call," "Explore collaboration." Redstone should embed CTAs after every major section: "Get a Free Quote" after capability sections, "Request Material Specs" after technical content, "Schedule a Plant Tour" after quality sections.

13

Consultative CTA Language (Not "Contact Us")

MEDIUM Effort: LOW

Omnius never says "Contact Us" as a CTA. It's always value-forward: "Explore how we can collaborate," "Partner with us." Redstone should replace "Contact Us" with: "Get Your Custom Quote," "Talk to an Engineer," "See How We Can Help," "Start Your Project."

14

Process Transparency Section

MEDIUM Effort: LOW

Omnius has a "Getting Started" section showing their process. Redstone should have a "How We Work" section on the homepage: Step 1: Share Your Specs -> Step 2: Engineering Review (48h) -> Step 3: Prototype Quote -> Step 4: Production. This reduces perceived risk for first-time buyers.

TIER 4: Technical SEO (Outdo Omnius)

15

Schema Markup (Where Omnius Fails)

HIGH Effort: MEDIUM

Omnius has zero schema. Redstone can beat them here. Add: Organization schema (homepage), LocalBusiness schema (contact), Product schema (each service page), FAQ schema (every page with Q&A content), BreadcrumbList schema (all pages), Review schema (testimonial pages). This unlocks rich snippets that Omnius doesn't have.

16

Featured Snippet Optimization

MEDIUM Effort: LOW

Omnius owns zero featured snippets. For manufacturing content, format definitions and comparisons as snippet-ready: "What is die casting?" answered in a 40-60 word paragraph at the top of relevant pages. Use comparison tables for "aluminum vs zinc" type queries. Google loves tables and concise definitions.

17

Complete Meta Description Coverage

MEDIUM Effort: LOW

Omnius is missing meta descriptions on key pages. Every Redstone page should have a unique, keyword-optimized meta description (150-160 chars) with a CTA verb ("Get a quote," "Learn how"). This is table-stakes SEO that Omnius is leaving on the table.

TIER 5: Things Omnius Doesn't Do (Redstone Advantages)

18

Video Content (Plant Tours, Process Videos)

HIGH Effort: MEDIUM

Omnius has zero video content. Manufacturing has a massive advantage here: actual physical processes are inherently visual and trustworthy. Record: plant tours, die casting process videos, quality inspection walkthroughs, engineer interviews. Embed on service pages and upload to YouTube for dual ranking.

19

Interactive Cost Calculator

HIGH Effort: HIGH

Omnius has no interactive tools. A "Manufacturing Cost Estimator" (material + volume + process -> rough price range) would be a lead magnet that competitors can't easily copy. It captures email + project details in exchange for an estimate. This is the highest-value content asset Redstone could build.

20

Geographic Trust Pages

MEDIUM Effort: LOW

Omnius has "UK SaaS SEO" in their footer - geo-targeted pages. Redstone should create: /manufacturing-in-mexico, /nearshore-manufacturing, /USMCA-manufacturing-benefits. These target "manufacturing in Mexico" and "nearshore manufacturing" queries that are growing fast with the reshoring trend.

21

Third-Party Review Profiles

MEDIUM Effort: LOW

Omnius has zero G2, Capterra, or Trustpilot presence. Redstone should claim profiles on: Google Business, Thomasnet (manufacturing-specific), Alibaba/GlobalSources (if applicable), industry directories, BBB. These create backlinks and third-party trust signals that Omnius completely lacks.

22

Certification & Compliance Content

MEDIUM Effort: LOW

Manufacturing has a content advantage SaaS agencies don't: certifications. Create dedicated pages for each cert: /iso-9001-certified, /iatf-16949, /nadca-member. These rank for "[certification] + manufacturing" queries and signal trust. Omnius can't replicate this because they don't have physical certifications.

23

Material Specification Library

MEDIUM Effort: MEDIUM

Create a searchable library of material specs (A380 aluminum, Zamak zinc alloys, 4140 steel, etc.) with properties, applications, and Redstone's capabilities for each. This is programmatic SEO for manufacturing - each material page targets "[material] + die casting" queries. Engineers searching for material specs will find Redstone and stay.
