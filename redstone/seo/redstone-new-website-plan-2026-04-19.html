01 / Why Rebuild

What's Wrong with the Current Site

The current WordPress site has been live for years and does get organic traffic. But it has structural problems that can't be fixed with patches.

The problems

No meta descriptionsZero pages have authored meta descriptions. Google is pulling random text from the page body. This directly hurts click-through rates from search.
No schema markup liveZero structured data on any of the 133 indexed URLs today. This means no rich results in Google (no FAQ dropdowns, no company panels, no star ratings).
Content integrity issuesThe China foundry page contains plastic injection molding content copy-pasted from another template. Other pages have similar bleed.
Blog is invisible85 blog posts exist but /blog/ is not linked from the main navigation. The "Posts" dropdown hardcodes 7 articles. New posts never appear in the nav.
No hub-and-spoke structureService pages don't link to related content. Blog posts don't link back to services. There's no internal linking strategy connecting them.
Rankings are decliningOrganic traffic peaked in February (389 sessions/week) and has dropped to 180 sessions/week in April. Average positions are drifting from page 1 to page 2-3.
Speed and designWordPress with plugins is slow. The design doesn't match the quality of Redstone's work. The new LPs we've built are faster, cleaner, and convert better.

What's working (and we'll keep)

Material spec pages35 pages like /st37-equivalent-materials-metal-specifications/ drive 60%+ of organic traffic. Engineers searching for material data find these and navigate to the contact page. These pages MUST be preserved with exact URL redirects.
Contact page converts316 organic sessions to /contact-us/ in 90 days with only 6% bounce rate. People who find you organically are reaching out.
Branded search is strong"redstone manufacturing" gets 295 clicks at position 2.4 with 50% CTR. The brand signal is healthy.
Country page structureThe Mexico/India/China/Brazil foundry page hierarchy is a good concept. We'll rebuild it cleaner.

How Pages Connect (Hub-and-Spoke)

Every service hub page links to its related blog articles, material specs, and industry pages. Those pages link back. This builds topical authority with Google. Example for die casting:

/die-casting (hub) links to:
  → /blog/aluminum-vs-zinc-die-casting (spoke)
  → /blog/die-casting-tolerances-guide (spoke)
  → /blog/die-casting-in-mexico-usmca (spoke)
  → /materials/a380-aluminum (material)
  → /automotive (industry cross-link)
  → /mexico (country cross-link)

Each spoke links back to /die-casting in the first paragraph.

05 / SEO Strategy

What We Deploy on the New Site

1. Schema markup on every page

Organization, LocalBusiness, Product, FAQ, and BreadcrumbList structured data on every page from day one. This is what gets you rich results in Google — FAQ dropdowns, company info panels, star ratings in search.

2. Meta descriptions on 100% of pages

Custom-written, keyword-optimized descriptions for every page. This is the text Google shows under your listing. Right now it's pulling random content from the page body. Fixing this alone could improve click-through rates 20-40%.

3. Hub-and-spoke internal linking

Every service page links to its related blog articles, material specs, and industry pages. Every article links back. This tells Google "Redstone is the authority on die casting" instead of having disconnected pages floating on their own.

4. Material spec library (your biggest organic asset)

The material specification pages drive 60% of your organic traffic. We rebuild them with better design, proper schema, and links to relevant services. An engineer reading about SS400 steel sees "Get a quote for SS400 die casting" right there on the page.

5. Industry pages (new traffic source)

Automotive, medical devices, aerospace, consumer electronics, industrial. A procurement manager at a medical device company searches "medical device casting" -- right now Redstone doesn't show up. These pages fix that.

6. Blog content (4-6 articles/month)

Educational content that supports the service hubs: "Aluminum vs Zinc Die Casting," "Nearshore Manufacturing Guide," "CNC Machining Tolerances Explained." Each article targets a specific keyword and links back to the relevant service page.

7. Fat footer with 30+ internal links

Four-column footer linking to every service, industry, country, and key resource page. Free internal link equity flowing to every important page on every pageview.
