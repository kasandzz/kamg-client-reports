What we want to ask you, with the data in hand

Each card is a pattern we noticed in the CSVs alone. None of these required logging into a single ad account. We're framing them as questions because the data tells us what's happening, not why, and the why is the conversation we want on the kickoff call.

01
Quick win
Would seeing in-shop vs mobile broken out monthly change any of your decisions?
Shop column = "Smith Bros Detail Shop"
→ 1,659 in-shop · 22,889 mobile
Roughly 93 / 7 split, every job, every month
You mentioned the monthly totals don't separate the two channels. They do, in your Fieldd data, in two columns at once (Shop and Miles Traveled). We can backfill the revenue split for every month historically without any new tagging work. The bigger question is whether that breakdown would actually shape how you allocate budget, hire, or expand.
On the call: Does anyone here track these two channels in their head as separate businesses already? What would seeing it on paper unlock?

Have you run win-back campaigns before, and what was the response?

On the call: Who owns the Fieldd to Klaviyo flow today, and how often does anyone look at it?

05
Tracking gap
Is job-level cost and margin something you'd want tracked, or has that been a deliberate skip?
24,548 jobs total
215 with Margin field filled (0.9%)
Cost data missing on virtually every job
Fieldd has a Cost field and a Margin field at the job level. They're almost never being filled in. So every "profit" number in the system today is the gross sale, which makes real unit-economics tough. A $400 in-shop detail with one tech and zero drive time isn't the same as a $400 mobile job with 45 minutes of drive each way and product cost. Has the data-entry overhead just not been worth it so far, or is there something else going on?
On the call: If you saw margin per job split by service line and channel, would that change any of your daily decisions?
06
Tracking gap
Is the current rate of post-job review capture intentional, or something you've been wanting to fix?
Jobs with a rating captured: 4,750 of 24,548 (19%)
Of those captured, 97% are five-star
4,608 five-star ratings already in your data
You sit at 950+ five-star public reviews across Google and Yelp. Your internal Fieldd rate of five-stars is 97% when captured. The 81% of jobs without a rating attached are the gap between where you are and where you could be on visible social proof. Has anyone tried to systematize the post-job review ask?
On the call: What's the current process for asking for a review after a job? Who does it, and on which platforms?
07

Top 10 customer LTV total: $213,532
Median customer LTV: $289

There's a VIP cohort hiding inside your CRM. Right now (as far as the booking data shows) they get the same touch as a one-and-done lead. A simple ranking by Total Billed gives a list of named people who could be on a different track entirely. Curious whether you already think of them as a distinct group, or whether they sit inside the general mix.
On the call: Does the team know who the top 50 customers are by name? What would you want to do for them that you're not doing yet?
08
Worth a look
Are your flash sales pulling forward demand, or training customers to wait for the next one?
145 unique discount codes in the dataset
40+ flash-sale codes by date
Pattern: weekly to twice-weekly flash promotions
Heavy promotional cadence can work for category-leading businesses with strong margin headroom, and it can also quietly teach customers to wait for the next discount. We don't know which side you're on yet, but we want to understand what's driven the cadence so far. Is it a fill-the-calendar move when bookings are soft, or a marketing program with intent behind it?
On the call: What's the decision rule that fires a new flash sale? Who calls it, and what triggers it?
